# What We're Looking For

I want someone who gets excited about turning complex technical concepts into stories people actually want to read. Not someone who thinks "content marketing" means churning out SEO-optimized blog posts that nobody reads.

Looking for curiosity over credentials. Someone who asks follow-up questions when engineers explain features, who actually uses our product before writing about it, and who understands that good content marketing starts with genuine empathy for the reader's problem.

This is a junior role, so I'm optimizing for learning velocity and growth mindset over years of experience. I'd rather hire someone who's written thoughtful technical content as a side project than someone with 3 years of generic B2B content experience.

## Must-Haves

- Writing samples that show ability to explain technical concepts clearly (blog posts, documentation, social media threads)
- Basic understanding of content distribution beyond "publish and pray" (email, social, community engagement)
- Genuine curiosity about how software products work and why people use them
- Can adapt tone and complexity for different audiences (developers vs. decision-makers)

## Strong Signals

- Has built something (personal project, side hustle) and written about the process
- Actively engages in relevant online communities (Twitter, Reddit, Discord, forums)
- Shows understanding that good content solves real problems, not just drives traffic
- Experience with basic content tools (CMS, email platforms, social scheduling)

## Anti-Patterns

- Portfolio full of generic "5 Tips for Better X" listicles
- Talks about "viral content" as a strategy rather than focusing on value
- Can't explain why they chose to write about specific topics
- Views content marketing as just "writing blog posts"
- No evidence of actually reading/consuming content in our industry

## Interview Focus

- Walk through their writing process for a technical topic they've covered
- How they research and validate content ideas
- Examples of feedback they've received and how they incorporated it
- What content in our space they actually read and why
