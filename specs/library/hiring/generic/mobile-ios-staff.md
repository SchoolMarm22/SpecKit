# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's lived through multiple iOS platform transitions and can guide our mobile strategy for the next 3-5 years. This isn't just about writing Swift — it's about being the technical voice for iOS across the entire engineering organization.

You should have strong opinions about iOS architecture patterns, have battle scars from App Store review processes, and understand how mobile engineering decisions ripple through product, backend, and infrastructure teams. I'm looking for someone who can zoom out from implementation details to ask whether we're building the right thing in the right way.

The team respects technical depth, but at this level, your impact comes from multiplying the effectiveness of 15+ engineers across iOS, Android, and backend teams.

## Must-Haves

- Architected iOS apps serving 1M+ users with complex state management (Core Data, Realm, or custom solutions)
- Led technical decisions that affected multiple teams (API design, shared libraries, build systems)
- Mentored senior engineers and can articulate your technical mentorship philosophy
- Deep understanding of iOS performance profiling and has optimized apps under memory/battery constraints
- Experience with large-scale iOS refactoring (SwiftUI migration, modularization, or similar)

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about iOS testing strategies beyond "we write unit tests"
- Can explain the business impact of their technical decisions, not just the technical elegance
- Experience with cross-platform concerns (shared networking layers, analytics SDKs, etc.)
- Has navigated App Store rejections and knows the review guidelines inside-out
- Understanding of mobile CI/CD complexity and has improved build times significantly

## Anti-Patterns

- Can't articulate why they made specific architectural choices beyond "it's a best practice"
- Focuses primarily on individual contribution rather than organizational impact
- Dismisses cross-platform frameworks without nuanced understanding of tradeoffs
- Treats mobile as an afterthought to web development rather than its own discipline
- Can't explain how their work connects to business metrics beyond app store ratings

## Interview Focus

- System design: how would you architect our iOS app for 10x user growth
- Technical leadership stories: times they influenced technical direction across teams
- Platform evolution: how they've adapted to major iOS changes (SwiftUI, iOS 14 widgets, etc.)
- Code review philosophy and how they've improved team code quality
- Conflict resolution: times they've navigated technical disagreements with senior stakeholders
