# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can take a feature spec and ship it without needing their hand held. Not someone who needs architecture designed for them, but also not someone who's going to reinvent dependency injection because they read a Medium article.

The sweet spot is an engineer who's lived through at least one major Android version migration, dealt with real performance problems in production, and understands that "it works on my Pixel" isn't sufficient testing. They should have opinions about app architecture that come from experience, not just from following tutorials.

## Must-Haves

- 3+ years shipping Android apps to production (not side projects)
- Solid understanding of Android lifecycle and can explain why it matters for real user scenarios
- Experience with modern Android architecture (MVVM, Repository pattern, or similar) - can articulate why they chose their approach
- Has debugged and fixed ANRs or crashes in production apps
- Comfortable with Kotlin (not just Java with Kotlin syntax)

## Strong Signals

- Has opinions about testing that go beyond "we should write tests"
- Experience with CI/CD for mobile (knows the pain of slow build times)
- Can talk about app performance optimization they've actually done
- Understanding of Android security model and has implemented proper data storage
- Has worked with product/design on feature requirements, not just implemented specs

## Anti-Patterns

- Only Flutter or React Native experience trying to pivot to native Android
- Can't explain the difference between Service and IntentService, or why you'd use WorkManager instead
- Thinks fragments are always bad because someone told them so
- Has never dealt with backwards compatibility beyond "minSdk 21"
- Treats every feature like it needs a new architecture pattern

## Interview Focus

- Architecture decisions: why they structured their last major feature the way they did
- Performance debugging: specific examples of slowness they investigated and fixed
- Cross-team collaboration: how they work with backend engineers when APIs don't match mobile needs
- Testing strategy: not just "do you write tests" but how they decide what to test
- Production ownership: how they handle user-reported issues or crashes
