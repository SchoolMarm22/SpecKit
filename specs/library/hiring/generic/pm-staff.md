# What We're Looking For

I need a product leader who thinks in systems, not features. At the staff level, you're not just building products — you're building the frameworks that other PMs use to build products. You should have strong opinions about product strategy that come from having seen your own strategies fail.

Most importantly, I need someone who can influence without authority across engineering, design, and business stakeholders. You'll be the PM that other PMs come to when they're stuck on hard tradeoffs. Technical depth matters here — you need to hold your own in architecture discussions and challenge engineering on scope vs. complexity tradeoffs.

## Must-Haves

- 7+ years PM experience with at least 2 years leading other PMs or influencing product direction across multiple teams
- Shipped complex, multi-quarter initiatives that required coordinating 3+ engineering teams
- Can articulate a product vision that connects user problems to business metrics to technical architecture
- Experience with both 0-to-1 products and scaling existing products (different muscles, both needed)
- Track record of making hard prioritization decisions under resource constraints

## Strong Signals

- Has built or significantly influenced product frameworks (experimentation, launch processes, metrics definitions)
- Can explain technical tradeoffs in depth — understands why the engineering team pushes back on seemingly simple requests
- Experience turning around a struggling product or team
- Has hired and developed other PMs

## Anti-Patterns

- Career spent only at large tech companies with infinite resources (hasn't had to make real tradeoffs)
- Can't explain why their past products succeeded beyond "great execution"
- Treats engineering like a development shop rather than strategic partners
- Focused on outputs (features shipped) rather than outcomes (user/business impact)
- Never been responsible for a product that failed or underperformed

## Interview Focus

- System-level thinking: how they approach problems that span multiple teams/products
- Influence without authority: specific examples of changing minds when they weren't the decision maker
- Technical judgment: can they reason about engineering complexity and make informed tradeoffs
- Failure stories: what they learned from products/features that didn't work
- Vision vs. execution: how they balance long-term strategy with immediate tactical needs
