# What We're Looking For

I need someone who thinks about lifecycle marketing as a growth engine, not a campaign factory. At staff level, you should be the person who redesigns our entire funnel based on cohort analysis, not just A/B testing subject lines.

You've built lifecycle programs that scaled from thousands to millions of users. You understand the technical constraints — deliverability, data infrastructure, cross-channel attribution — and can work directly with engineering to solve them. When our email platform hits rate limits or our CDP can't handle the segmentation logic you want, you know how to prioritize the technical work needed to unlock growth.

Most importantly, you've mentored other marketers and can teach the difference between tactics and strategy. Junior marketers optimize open rates; staff marketers optimize customer lifetime value through systematic funnel improvements.

## Must-Haves

- Built and scaled lifecycle programs for 1M+ user products (B2C or high-volume B2B)
- Owned end-to-end funnel optimization with measurable impact on LTV/CAC
- Designed segmentation strategies that required custom data infrastructure or CDP work
- Led cross-functional initiatives with engineering, analytics, and product teams
- Mentored 3+ marketers and can articulate your framework for lifecycle strategy

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about email deliverability and sender reputation management
- Experience with complex multi-touch attribution models, not just last-click
- Built retention programs that actually moved retention curves, not just engagement metrics
- Can speak fluently about SQL, APIs, and data modeling constraints
- Has redesigned an entire lifecycle program based on cohort behavior analysis

## Anti-Patterns

- "Lifecycle" experience that was really just email blast campaigns
- Can't explain the technical reasons behind deliverability best practices
- Measures success only in channel metrics (open rates, CTR) rather than business outcomes
- Never worked with engineering teams on marketing infrastructure
- Treats personalization as just dynamic content insertion rather than behavioral segmentation

## Interview Focus

- System redesign stories: how they rebuilt a lifecycle program and why those decisions drove better outcomes
- Technical fluency: can they explain why certain segmentation logic breaks at scale
- Cross-functional influence: examples of getting engineering resources for marketing infrastructure
- Mentorship philosophy: how they teach other marketers to think strategically rather than tactically
