# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can own a growth channel end-to-end, not just execute campaigns someone else designed. At the mid-level, you should be forming hypotheses, running experiments, and making data-driven decisions without constant direction.

Most "growth marketers" I interview have run Facebook ads and called it growth. Real growth marketing means understanding your funnel, knowing your unit economics, and being able to connect acquisition tactics to retention outcomes. If you can't explain why your CAC went up last quarter or what levers actually move your north star metric, this isn't the role for you.

## Must-Haves

- Owned a growth channel with meaningful budget ($50K+ monthly spend)
- Built and optimized conversion funnels with measurable lift (not just A/B tested button colors)
- SQL proficiency for customer segmentation and cohort analysis
- Experience with attribution modeling beyond last-click (MMM, MTA, or incrementality testing)
- Can calculate and optimize for LTV:CAC ratios across different cohorts

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about iOS 14.5 impact and how they adapted attribution
- Built automated reporting dashboards (Looker, Tableau, or similar)
- Experience with lifecycle marketing tools beyond just blast emails
- Can articulate why they chose specific channels based on customer profiles, not just "Facebook worked"

## Anti-Patterns

- Growth experience limited to paid social ads with no funnel optimization
- Can't explain the difference between correlation and causation in their results
- Treats "growth hacking" as tactics over systematic experimentation
- Never owned P&L responsibility or budget allocation decisions
- Can't connect their work to actual business outcomes beyond vanity metrics

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a failed experiment and what they learned from it
- How they think about incrementality vs. attribution in their current channels
- Their framework for prioritizing growth opportunities when resources are limited
- Technical depth on their analytics stack and how they ensure data quality
