# What We're Looking For

I'm looking for someone early in their career who's genuinely curious about infrastructure and has gotten their hands dirty with the fundamentals. This isn't about years of experience — it's about whether you've actually run things in production, even if it was just a personal project that got real traffic.

The best junior DevOps engineers I've hired were the ones who broke something, fixed it, and then built monitoring so it wouldn't break again. I care more about your troubleshooting instincts and willingness to dig into problems than your familiarity with our specific tools.

## Must-Haves

- Has deployed and maintained at least one real application (even if it's a side project with actual users)
- Comfortable with Linux command line — can navigate, debug processes, read logs without a GUI
- Experience with at least one cloud provider (AWS, GCP, Azure) beyond just following tutorials
- Has written scripts or automation that someone else actually uses
- Can explain what happens when you type a URL into a browser (networking fundamentals matter)

## Strong Signals

- Built something with Docker that wasn't just following a getting-started guide
- Has opinions about monitoring based on actual experience debugging issues
- Contributed to open source projects or has public code repositories
- Self-taught most of their technical skills (shows learning ability)
- Has broken production and learned from it

## Anti-Patterns

- Only "DevOps" experience is from bootcamp labs or coursework with no real users
- Thinks DevOps just means "using Docker and AWS"
- Can't articulate why they chose one tool over another for a specific use case
- Expects to learn everything on the job without self-directed exploration
- Views infrastructure as just "making things work" rather than understanding the why

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a technical problem they've actually solved (the debugging process matters more than the solution)
- How they approach learning new technologies — do they just follow tutorials or do they experiment?
- What questions they ask about our infrastructure challenges (curiosity is key)
- Their understanding of basic concepts: load balancing, databases, networking, security fundamentals
