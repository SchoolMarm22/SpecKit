# What We're Looking For

I need someone who understands that platform engineering isn't just "DevOps with better marketing." You should have strong opinions about developer experience because you've felt the pain of bad tooling yourself. The best platform engineers I've worked with started as software engineers who got tired of waiting for infrastructure tickets.

We're building internal platforms that 40+ engineers will use daily. That means your APIs need to be as thoughtfully designed as any customer-facing product. You'll be making architectural decisions that affect every deployment, every environment spin-up, every developer's daily workflow. I want someone who's lived through the consequences of their infrastructure choices at scale.

## Must-Haves

- Built and operated production platforms used by 20+ engineers
- Deep experience with container orchestration (Kubernetes preferred, but solid Docker Swarm or ECS experience counts)
- Designed and implemented CI/CD pipelines that handle 50+ deployments per week
- Infrastructure as Code expertise with Terraform, CloudFormation, or Pulumi at enterprise scale
- Real production incident response experience, including being the person who gets called at 2am

## Strong Signals

- Has migrated a significant system between cloud providers or architectures
- Built internal developer tooling that other engineers actually wanted to use
- Experience with service mesh, GitOps, or advanced Kubernetes patterns
- Can articulate the business impact of infrastructure decisions, not just the technical elegance
- Has mentored other engineers on infrastructure topics

## Anti-Patterns

- "Senior" experience that was really just maintaining someone else's infrastructure
- Can't explain why they chose specific tools beyond "it's what the team was already using"
- Treats security and compliance as afterthoughts rather than foundational requirements
- Has never had to debug a production issue in a system they didn't originally build
- Focuses on automation for automation's sake without measuring developer productivity impact

## Interview Focus

- Architecture decisions: what they built, why they built it that way, what they'd change knowing what they know now
- How they balance developer velocity with operational stability
- Their approach to capacity planning and cost optimization
- Specific examples of improving developer experience through infrastructure changes
- How they handle technical disagreements with other senior engineers
