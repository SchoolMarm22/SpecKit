# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's been through the full growth lifecycle — not just the hockey stick phase everyone talks about, but the messy middle where CAC creeps up and LTV models break down. You should have war stories about channels that stopped working, attribution models that lied to you, and growth experiments that seemed brilliant until they weren't.

This isn't a "spray and pray" marketing role. I want someone who thinks like a product manager but executes like a marketer. You should be comfortable owning a P&L, arguing with engineers about tracking implementation, and explaining to the CEO why our attribution is showing 140% of revenue coming from paid channels.

The team is small but the scope is huge — you'll own everything from lifecycle email to paid acquisition to conversion optimization. We need someone who can build systems, not just run campaigns.

## Must-Haves

- Owned full-funnel growth at a B2B SaaS company with >$5M ARR
- Built and managed multi-channel attribution models (UTMs aren't enough)
- Scaled a paid channel from $10k to $100k+ monthly spend while maintaining or improving unit economics
- Led growth experiments with statistical rigor — knows when to call a test and why
- Experience with modern growth stack: Segment/mParticle, Mixpanel/Amplitude, and can work with engineering on tracking implementation

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about incrementality testing vs. attribution modeling
- Can explain why they chose their current marketing mix model
- Experience with lifecycle marketing beyond just drip campaigns
- Has built or managed a referral program that actually worked
- Understanding of how product changes impact growth metrics

## Anti-Patterns

- Growth experience that was really just paid ads optimization
- Can't explain the difference between correlation and causation in their experiments
- Has never dealt with iOS 14.5+ attribution challenges
- Thinks growth is just about top-of-funnel metrics
- Has only worked with massive budgets or company-defining tailwinds

## Interview Focus

- Channel strategy decisions: why they prioritized what they did, what they deprioritized, and how they measured success
- Failed experiment stories — what they learned and how they applied it
- How they've worked with product teams to improve activation and retention
- Their approach to incrementality testing and dealing with attribution gaps
- Cross-functional leadership examples, especially with engineering and product
