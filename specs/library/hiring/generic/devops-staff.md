# What We're Looking For

I need someone who thinks about infrastructure as a product with internal customers, not just a collection of servers to keep running. At staff level, you should be driving technical strategy across multiple teams and thinking 2-3 quarters ahead about what our platform needs to support.

You've probably been the person who got called when the entire engineering org was blocked by infrastructure limitations. More importantly, you've been the person who prevented those situations by building systems that scale ahead of demand and designing for failure from day one.

This isn't a "senior with more years" role — this is about organizational impact. You should have war stories about migrating critical systems with zero downtime, building platforms that enabled 5x engineering team growth, or designing disaster recovery that actually worked when tested in production.

## Must-Haves

- Architected and executed multi-quarter infrastructure migrations affecting >20 engineers
- Built platform abstractions that eliminated entire classes of operational overhead for product teams
- Led incident response for business-critical outages and drove long-term reliability improvements
- Experience scaling infrastructure through at least one order of magnitude growth (10x users, traffic, or data)
- Proficiency in at least two IaC tools at enterprise scale (Terraform, Pulumi, CDK) with strong opinions on when to use which

## Strong Signals

- Has successfully migrated between cloud providers or significant platform changes (k8s versions, database engines, etc.)
- Built or significantly improved developer tooling that measurably improved engineering productivity
- Experience with FinOps or infrastructure cost optimization at scale — understands the business impact of technical decisions
- Can articulate infrastructure trade-offs in business terms, not just technical ones
- Has mentored other infrastructure engineers through complex technical decisions

## Anti-Patterns

- Describes their impact in terms of technologies used rather than problems solved
- Can't explain how their infrastructure decisions affected product development velocity
- Treats security and compliance as afterthoughts rather than design constraints
- Has only worked in environments where "throwing more resources at it" was always the solution
- Can't articulate the difference between reliability engineering and just "keeping things running"

## Interview Focus

- System design at organization scale: how do you design platforms for teams you don't directly work with?
- Migration strategy: walk through a complex migration you've led, including rollback plans and risk mitigation
- Technical leadership examples: how have you influenced technical decisions across multiple teams?
- Platform thinking: how do you balance platform flexibility vs. standardization?
- Business alignment: how do you prioritize infrastructure work against product roadmap pressure?
