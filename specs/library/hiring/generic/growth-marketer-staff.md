# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's been the sole growth person at a 50-person company *and* led growth at scale. Staff means you're not just running experiments — you're architecting the entire growth function. You should have opinions about attribution models, not just conversion funnels.

The difference between senior and staff growth is the difference between "this campaign performed well" and "here's why our entire acquisition strategy needs to change, and here's the 18-month roadmap to get there." You need to think in systems, not just tactics.

## Must-Haves

- Built a growth function from scratch (0 to meaningful revenue scale)
- Designed and implemented multi-touch attribution across 3+ channels
- Led cross-functional initiatives that required buy-in from eng, product, and data teams
- Shipped growth experiments that required non-trivial engineering work
- P&L ownership or direct revenue accountability (not just "influenced pipeline")

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about incrementality testing vs. A/B testing
- Can articulate why their last company's LTV:CAC ratio was what it was
- Experience with growth in multiple channels (paid, organic, lifecycle, referral)
- Built or significantly influenced product features for growth
- Has managed other growth marketers or cross-functional growth teams

## Anti-Patterns

- "Growth" experience that was really just paid media optimization
- Can't explain their attribution model or why they chose it
- Talks about "growth hacks" instead of sustainable systems
- Never had to justify budget allocation across channels
- Only worked at companies with product-market fit already established

## Interview Focus

- System design: how would you structure growth for a company transitioning from product-led to sales-led
- Attribution philosophy: how they think about incrementality vs. correlation, especially across online/offline channels
- Technical partnership: specific examples of working with engineering on growth infrastructure
- Resource allocation: how they'd prioritize a $2M annual growth budget across channels and why
- Failure analysis: growth bets that didn't work and how they changed their framework
