# What We're Looking For

I'm looking for someone who's hungry to learn and has strong design fundamentals, even if their product experience is limited. The best junior designers I've hired came from bootcamps, self-taught backgrounds, or adjacent fields like graphic design or psychology — what matters is how they think about problems, not where they learned Figma.

This role will involve a lot of learning on the job. You'll pair with senior designers, sit in user research sessions, and gradually take ownership of smaller features. I care more about your process and curiosity than your portfolio's polish.

## Must-Haves

- Strong visual design fundamentals (typography, spacing, hierarchy, color)
- Can articulate design decisions beyond "it looks good" — understands the "why"
- Proficiency in Figma including components, auto-layout, and basic prototyping
- Some experience with user-centered design thinking, even from class projects or bootcamp work
- Portfolio shows process, not just final pixels — sketches, iterations, thinking

## Strong Signals

- Has redesigned something poorly designed in their own life (an app, a process, anything)
- Asks thoughtful questions about user needs and business constraints
- Shows work that demonstrates iteration and learning from feedback
- Experience with user research, even informal (surveys, interviews, usability testing)

## Anti-Patterns

- Portfolio is all visual design with no UX thinking or process shown
- Can't explain why they made specific design choices
- Expects to work in isolation without feedback or collaboration
- Focuses only on aesthetics without considering usability or accessibility
- Defensive about critique or unwilling to iterate

## Interview Focus

- Design critique skills: can they identify good/bad design and explain why?
- Problem-solving approach: how do they break down ambiguous problems?
- Learning mindset: how do they handle not knowing something?
- Collaboration readiness: comfort with feedback, asking questions, working with developers
