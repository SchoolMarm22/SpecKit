# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's moved beyond just writing Node.js services to thinking about how Node.js services fit into larger distributed systems. At staff level, you should have opinions about when to choose Node vs. Go vs. Python for different workloads, and be able to articulate those tradeoffs to other engineers and product leaders.

You've likely been the person other engineers come to when they're stuck on architecture decisions. Maybe you've designed the event-driven system that handles millions of webhooks, or you're the one who figured out why that Express app was mysteriously leaking memory in production. Staff engineers at our level own outcomes, not just outputs — you think in terms of business impact, not just code shipped.

The role involves significant cross-team collaboration. You'll influence technical decisions across multiple services owned by different teams, and help establish patterns that other engineers will follow for years. If you've never had to convince someone to adopt your design when you had no formal authority over them, this probably isn't the right fit yet.

## Must-Haves

- 7+ years of production Node.js at scale (>1M requests/day)
- Designed and owned a distributed system with at least 5 interconnected services
- Led a major architectural migration or platform change that took 6+ months
- Experience mentoring senior engineers and influencing technical direction across teams
- Deep understanding of Node.js performance characteristics, event loop, and memory management

## Strong Signals

- Has written internal frameworks or libraries adopted by multiple teams
- Experience with Node.js in different contexts: APIs, background workers, real-time systems
- Strong opinions about service boundaries and data modeling backed by real experience
- Can speak to observability as a design concern, not an afterthought
- Has been on-call for systems they didn't write and improved them

## Anti-Patterns

- "Staff" experience that was really just senior-level work with a fancy title
- Can't articulate clear technical opinions or defaults ("it depends" without specifics)
- Has only worked on CRUD APIs — never dealt with complex state management or async workflows
- Treats TypeScript as optional or can't explain when they'd choose dynamic vs. static typing
- Never had to live with long-term consequences of their architectural decisions

## Interview Focus

- System design at realistic scale: how they'd evolve our current architecture over 2-3 years
- Technical leadership stories: times they influenced outcomes without direct authority
- Node.js depth: memory profiling, cluster management, worker threads, streams
- Cross-functional collaboration: how they've worked with product, data, and infrastructure teams
- Learning and teaching: how they stay current and help others level up
