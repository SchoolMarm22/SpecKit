# What We're Looking For

We need someone who thinks beyond features and thinks about the mobile platform as a system. At staff level, you're not just building screens — you're defining how we build screens for the next three years.

I want someone who's lived through the pain of technical debt at scale. Who's seen a beautiful architecture slowly decay under product pressure, and has opinions about how to prevent that. Someone who can look at our current Android app and see not just what it is, but what it needs to become as we scale from 50 to 500 engineers touching mobile code.

You should be the person other Android engineers come to when they're stuck on hard problems. Not because you know every API, but because you know how to think through complex technical tradeoffs and communicate those decisions to both engineers and leadership.

## Must-Haves

- 7+ years Android development with at least 2 years at staff/principal level
- Designed and led implementation of major architectural changes (modularization, navigation overhaul, build system migration)
- Experience defining mobile development standards across multiple teams
- Track record of mentoring senior engineers and influencing technical direction beyond their immediate team
- Deep understanding of Android performance optimization and can articulate memory management, rendering pipeline, and threading model tradeoffs

## Strong Signals

- Has opinions about Gradle that go beyond "it's slow" — can explain module boundaries, build optimization strategies, and dependency management at scale
- Experience with cross-platform architecture decisions (not necessarily implementation, but strategic thinking about when/why/how)
- Has driven adoption of new technologies or patterns across an engineering organization
- Can speak to mobile-specific product analytics and experimentation challenges

## Anti-Patterns

- Strong technical skills but has never influenced decisions outside their immediate team
- Dismisses newer Android patterns (Compose, Jetpack libraries) without understanding the problems they solve
- Can't articulate the business impact of their technical decisions
- "Staff engineer" experience that was really just senior engineer work with a fancy title
- Focuses on individual contributor work without considering organizational leverage

## Interview Focus

- System design: How would you architect our mobile app to support 10x engineering team growth?
- Technical strategy: Walk through a major architectural decision they've driven and how they built consensus
- Mentorship approach: How do they help senior engineers grow into staff-level thinking?
- Cross-functional influence: Examples of shaping product or design decisions through technical expertise
- Legacy system evolution: How they balance maintaining existing systems while driving toward future architecture
