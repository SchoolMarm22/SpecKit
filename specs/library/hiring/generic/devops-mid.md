# What We're Looking For

Looking for someone who's moved beyond following runbooks to actually writing them. You should have scars from at least one major incident where you were the primary responder, not just watching over someone's shoulder.

This isn't an entry-level "learn Kubernetes on the job" role, but it's also not a "design the entire platform architecture" role. You'll own specific services and tools, improve our developer experience, and be the go-to person for a few key areas of our infrastructure. The sweet spot is someone who can independently solve problems but knows when to escalate or collaborate.

## Must-Haves

- 2+ years managing production infrastructure with real traffic and real consequences
- Hands-on experience with containerization (Docker) and orchestration (Kubernetes or equivalent)
- Built and maintained CI/CD pipelines that other engineers actually use
- Infrastructure as Code experience (Terraform, CloudFormation, or similar) beyond basic tutorials
- On-call experience where you've been woken up and had to fix something

## Strong Signals

- Has improved deployment times or reduced build failures at a previous job
- Can explain monitoring decisions: why they chose certain metrics, thresholds, and alert conditions
- Experience with at least one major cloud provider (AWS, GCP, Azure) beyond basic VM management
- Has mentored junior engineers or new team members on infrastructure topics

## Anti-Patterns

- "DevOps" experience that was really just using Jenkins someone else configured
- Can't explain the difference between monitoring, logging, and observability
- Has never been responsible for infrastructure costs or resource optimization
- Thinks "it works on my machine" is an acceptable answer for deployment issues
- Only comfortable with GUI tools, can't work effectively from command line

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a recent infrastructure problem they solved independently
- How they approach troubleshooting: methodology, tools, and escalation decisions
- Their experience with infrastructure trade-offs (cost vs performance, reliability vs speed)
- Understanding of security basics: how they think about secrets management, network security, access controls
