# What We're Looking For

I'm looking for someone who thinks like a product person but doesn't necessarily have the title yet. Maybe you've been the "product-minded engineer" on your team, or the analyst who kept asking "but why would users want this?" You understand that building features is easy — building the right features is hard.

This is a true junior role. I don't need you to have run a P&L or launched a major product. I need you to show curiosity about user problems, comfort with ambiguity, and the ability to think in tradeoffs. You'll be working closely with a senior PM who will help you develop product instincts, but you need to come with the raw materials.

## Must-Haves

- Has talked to users (formally or informally) and changed something based on what they learned
- Can articulate a product decision they influenced, even in a small way
- Comfortable with basic SQL and data analysis — you don't need to be an analyst, but you need to be dangerous
- Experience working cross-functionally (eng, design, marketing, sales — any combination counts)

## Strong Signals

- Side projects or personal products that show product thinking
- Has strong opinions about products they use daily and can explain why
- Internship or project experience where they had to prioritize competing requests
- Customer support or sales experience (shows user empathy)

## Anti-Patterns

- Thinks product management is "telling engineers what to build"
- Can't explain why they want to be in product vs. staying in their current function
- Focuses only on features, not outcomes or user problems
- Treats data as confirmation rather than exploration
- Never says "I don't know" — junior PMs who pretend to have all the answers are dangerous

## Interview Focus

- Product sense: give them a product they don't know and have them critique it
- User empathy: how do they think about user problems vs. feature requests
- Prioritization instincts: simple tradeoff scenarios, not complex roadmapping
- Learning orientation: what product blogs/books/podcasts do they follow
