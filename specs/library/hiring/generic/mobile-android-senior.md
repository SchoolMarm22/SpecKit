# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's lived through the entire Android ecosystem evolution — from Eclipse to Android Studio, from AsyncTask to coroutines, from support libraries to Jetpack. Not because legacy knowledge is valuable, but because someone who's adapted through all these changes has developed architectural judgment.

The mobile team is small but our impact is huge. You'll be making decisions that affect millions of users' daily experience. I'm looking for someone who understands that "it works on my Pixel" isn't good enough when you're shipping to devices with 2GB RAM running Android 8.

This isn't a feature factory role. You'll spend as much time in architecture reviews and mentoring as you will writing code.

## Must-Haves

- 5+ years shipping production Android apps with 100k+ users
- Deep experience with modern Android architecture (MVVM, Clean Architecture, or similar patterns you can defend)
- Proficiency with Kotlin coroutines and Flow for async programming
- Has owned and improved app performance — can talk about specific optimizations you made and their measurable impact
- Experience mentoring junior/mid-level engineers with specific examples

## Strong Signals

- Built or significantly contributed to a custom UI component library
- Has shipped features using Jetpack Compose in production
- Understanding of Android's memory model and has debugged memory leaks
- Experience with modularization strategies and build optimization
- Can articulate when to use LiveData vs Flow vs RxJava and why

## Anti-Patterns

- Only worked with small teams where "senior" meant "writes the most code"
- Can't explain Android lifecycle edge cases beyond the happy path
- Architectural opinions that are just "what we used at my last company"
- Has never been responsible for app store ratings or crash-free sessions
- Dismisses cross-platform as "not real mobile development"

## Interview Focus

- Architecture decisions: walk through a complex feature they designed and why they made specific choices
- Performance debugging: real examples of performance problems they've solved
- Team leadership: how they've influenced technical direction beyond their own code
- Platform knowledge: edge cases in Android development that reveal depth of experience
- Mentoring approach: how they help junior engineers grow beyond just code reviews
