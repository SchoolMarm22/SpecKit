# What We're Looking For

I need someone who thinks in systems, not campaigns. At this level, you should be designing lifecycle experiences that span months, not optimizing individual sends. You've built email programs that generated real revenue — not just engagement metrics that look good in a quarterly review.

The right person has been burned by bad data hygiene and knows why deliverability isn't just "avoiding spam filters." They've lived through a major ESP migration, dealt with iOS Mail Privacy Protection destroying their open rates, and can articulate why batch-and-blast is marketing malpractice. Most importantly, they can teach others why these things matter.

## Must-Haves

- Built and owned a complete lifecycle program (onboarding → activation → retention → winback) that demonstrably moved business metrics
- Deep technical knowledge of email deliverability — can explain DKIM, SPF, DMARC and has managed sender reputation through domain warming
- Advanced segmentation and personalization beyond "Hi [FirstName]" — dynamic content, behavioral triggers, predictive modeling
- Experience with marketing automation platforms (Klaviyo, Braze, Iterable) including custom event tracking and API integrations
- Led cross-functional projects with Engineering and Product to implement lifecycle improvements

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about list growth tactics and can explain why most lead magnets are garbage
- Experience with email rendering across clients and can discuss the pain of Outlook's Word rendering engine
- Built or improved email template systems and understands the tradeoffs between flexibility and consistency
- Can discuss attribution models and knows why last-touch attribution kills lifecycle programs

## Anti-Patterns

- Measures success primarily through open rates and click rates instead of business outcomes
- "Growth hacking" mentality that prioritizes volume over value
- Can't explain why their unsubscribe rate is what it is or treats high unsubscribes as purely negative
- Views email as a broadcast channel rather than a conversation medium
- Has never dealt with deliverability issues because they've only worked with small, clean lists

## Interview Focus

- Program architecture: how they think about mapping customer journey stages to email touchpoints
- Technical depth: can they troubleshoot deliverability issues and explain complex automation logic
- Business impact: specific revenue or retention numbers they've driven, and how they measured attribution
- Team development: how they've elevated junior marketers' technical skills and strategic thinking
