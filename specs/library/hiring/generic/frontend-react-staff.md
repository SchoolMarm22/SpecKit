# What We're Looking For

We need someone who can see around corners. Staff engineers don't just build features — they build the systems and processes that let teams build features faster, safer, and with less cognitive overhead. You should have strong opinions about frontend architecture that come from having been burned by your own past decisions.

This isn't about being the best individual contributor. It's about being a force multiplier. Can you design a component API that prevents entire classes of bugs? Can you introduce tooling that makes the whole team 20% more productive? Can you have the hard conversation about technical debt that everyone else is avoiding?

You'll be working across teams, not just within one. Frontend at this level means understanding how your decisions ripple through the entire product organization.

## Must-Haves

- Designed and evolved component systems used by multiple teams (50+ components, not just a few shared buttons)
- Led a major frontend migration or refactor that took 6+ months and involved multiple engineers
- Experience with frontend performance at scale — can speak to bundle optimization, lazy loading, and real user monitoring
- Has built or significantly improved developer experience tooling (build systems, testing infrastructure, local dev setup)
- Track record of technical writing and cross-team communication (RFCs, architectural decisions, postmortems)

## Strong Signals

- Has opinions about when NOT to use React (and can defend them)
- Experience with micro-frontends or other strategies for scaling large frontend codebases
- Built accessibility into systems, not just individual components
- Has mentored senior engineers, not just junior ones
- Can articulate the business impact of technical decisions

## Anti-Patterns

- "Staff" experience that was really senior IC work with a fancier title
- Can't explain technical tradeoffs in business terms
- Architect who hasn't written production code in the last year
- Dismisses "junior" concerns like bundle size or developer ergonomics
- Never disagreed with a product decision and pushed back effectively

## Interview Focus

- System design: How would you structure a frontend for a team of 50 engineers?
- Technical strategy: Walk through a major architectural decision you drove and why you'd approach it differently today
- Cross-team influence: How do you get buy-in for technical initiatives when you don't have authority?
- Developer experience: What's the most impactful tooling or process improvement you've shipped?
