# What We're Looking For

I need someone who has lived through the full attribution nightmare — not just pulled reports, but actually architected measurement frameworks that executives trust. You should have scars from failed attribution models and strong opinions about why most companies get incrementality wrong.

The data landscape is littered with analysts who can write SQL but can't explain why our CAC went up 40% last month in a way that actually helps the CMO make decisions. I want someone who thinks like a marketer but codes like an engineer.

## Must-Haves

- Built multi-touch attribution models from scratch (not just configured tools)
- Designed and executed incrementality tests (geo tests, holdouts, synthetic controls)
- Owned P&L impact measurement for a marketing budget >$10M annually
- Advanced SQL with window functions, CTEs, and query optimization experience
- Led cross-functional projects with marketing, product, and engineering teams

## Strong Signals

- Experience with modern data stack (dbt, Airflow, Snowflake/BigQuery)
- Has strong opinions about MTA vs MMM and can defend their choice
- Built dashboards that actually changed marketing strategy (not just reported metrics)
- Statistical background with A/B testing and causal inference
- Experience with ad platform APIs for data ingestion

## Anti-Patterns

- "Analytics" experience that was mostly dashboard maintenance
- Can't explain the difference between correlation and causation in attribution
- Treats marketing channels in isolation instead of understanding interaction effects
- Relies entirely on last-click attribution without questioning it
- Can't translate statistical concepts for non-technical stakeholders

## Interview Focus

- Attribution war stories: what broke, why it broke, how they fixed measurement
- How they've influenced marketing strategy through analysis (specific examples)
- Their approach to incrementality testing and why most companies do it wrong
- Technical depth: can they architect a data pipeline, not just consume one
