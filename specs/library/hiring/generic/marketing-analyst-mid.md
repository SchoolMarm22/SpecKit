# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can own a marketing channel's analytics end-to-end without me checking their work every week. You should be past the "learning SQL basics" phase and into "I built this attribution model and here's why it's better than last-touch."

The best mid-level analysts I've worked with have strong opinions about data quality and can spot when campaign performance suddenly looks too good to be true. They don't just run reports — they ask why the numbers changed and actually dig until they find the answer.

## Must-Haves

- 2-3 years analyzing marketing performance with SQL (not just Excel pivots)
- Built and maintained marketing attribution models beyond first/last-touch
- Experience with at least two major ad platforms (Facebook, Google, LinkedIn) including their APIs or bulk exports
- Can translate marketing metrics into business impact — knows the difference between vanity metrics and what actually drives revenue
- Has debugged tracking issues (UTM problems, pixel fires, conversion delays)

## Strong Signals

- Experience with a marketing data stack (Segment, Stitch, Fivetran + warehouse)
- Has opinions about incrementality testing and can explain why correlation isn't causation
- Built automated reporting that other teams actually use
- Experience with cohort analysis or customer lifetime value modeling

## Anti-Patterns

- Only experience is with pre-built dashboards or marketing platform reporting
- Can't explain why they'd choose one attribution model over another
- Treats every metric spike or drop as normal without investigating
- Has never questioned data quality or found a tracking bug
- Talks about "insights" but can't connect them to specific business decisions

## Interview Focus

- Walk me through a time when campaign performance data didn't make sense and how you figured out what was wrong
- How do you decide what to measure for a new marketing channel?
- Describe a marketing experiment you designed and what you learned from it
- What's your approach to measuring channels that don't have perfect tracking (like podcast ads or billboards)?
