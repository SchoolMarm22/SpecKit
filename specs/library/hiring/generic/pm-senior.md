# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's been in the room when a product decision went sideways and had to own the fix. Too many PMs I interview have great frameworks and beautiful roadmaps but have never had to tell engineering "actually, we need to kill this feature we've been building for three months."

The best senior PMs I've worked with are comfortable being wrong, but more importantly, they're fast at recognizing when they're wrong. They've built products that failed and can tell you exactly why, without blaming the market or "lack of resources."

## Must-Haves

- Shipped a 0-to-1 product that got real user adoption (>10k MAU)
- Led a product pivot or major feature kill based on data, not politics
- Has worked directly with engineering teams of 5+ people on technical roadmaps
- Can analyze user behavior data and translate it into specific product decisions
- Experience managing up to executives with conflicting priorities

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about what metrics actually matter vs. vanity metrics
- Can tell war stories about cross-functional conflict they resolved
- Experience with both B2B and B2C products (shows adaptability)
- Has hired and mentored junior PMs

## Anti-Patterns

- "Product success" that was really driven by sales or marketing
- Can't defend their prioritization framework when challenged
- Treats engineering estimates as commitments rather than starting points
- Uses "user research" to mean "I talked to three customers once"
- Has never had to cut scope to hit a deadline

## Interview Focus

- Product failure stories: what happened, what they learned, how they'd approach it now
- How they handle technical debt conversations with engineering
- Their framework for saying no to feature requests from executives
- Specific examples of using data to change stakeholder minds
