# What We're Looking For

I need someone who has moved beyond tutorials and Kaggle competitions into production ML. You should have shipped at least one model that real users interact with, even if it was "just" a recommendation engine or fraud detection system.

The key difference I'm looking for at this level is independent execution. I don't want to review every line of your feature engineering code or explain why you need to version your datasets. You should know when to reach for deep learning vs. when a simple logistic regression will solve the problem just fine.

## Must-Haves

- Deployed at least one ML model to production and monitored its performance over time
- Solid grasp of ML fundamentals: can explain bias-variance tradeoff, cross-validation, and when regularization matters without Googling
- Experience with the full ML pipeline: data preprocessing, feature engineering, model training, evaluation, and deployment
- Comfortable with both Python ML stack (pandas, scikit-learn, numpy) and at least one deep learning framework (PyTorch or TensorFlow)
- Can write clean, readable code that other engineers can understand and maintain

## Strong Signals

- Has experience with A/B testing ML models (knows the difference between offline metrics and business impact)
- Can articulate why they chose specific algorithms for past projects beyond "it had the highest accuracy"
- Experience with MLOps tools like MLflow, Weights & Biases, or similar
- Has dealt with data drift or model degradation in production

## Anti-Patterns

- Only experience is from online courses or competitions with clean datasets
- Can't explain their model choices or just says "I tried everything and this worked best"
- Thinks more complex models are always better (the "let's use a transformer for everything" syndrome)
- Has never worked with messy, real-world data or doesn't understand data quality issues
- Treats model accuracy as the only metric that matters

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a project end-to-end: how did you frame the problem, what data challenges did you face, why those specific models?
- Production experience: how do you monitor model performance, what happens when a model starts degrading?
- Technical depth on fundamentals: can they explain overfitting, feature selection, evaluation metrics for imbalanced datasets?
- Collaboration skills: how do they translate business requirements into ML problems, how do they communicate model limitations to non-technical stakeholders?
