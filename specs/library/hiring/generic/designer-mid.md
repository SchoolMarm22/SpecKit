# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can own a feature end-to-end without constant direction. Not the entire product strategy — that's still my job — but they should be able to take a problem statement and ship a solution without me reviewing every wireframe.

The sweet spot is 3-5 years of experience where they've moved beyond just making things pretty and started thinking about user flows, edge cases, and how their designs actually get built. I want someone who's been in enough design critiques to give and receive feedback professionally, and who's worked closely enough with engineers to know when they're designing something impossible.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped at least 2 major features from concept to production
- Can run user research sessions (interviews, usability tests) independently
- Proficient in Figma with component libraries and design systems
- Experience collaborating with engineering during implementation
- Portfolio shows clear problem definition, process, and outcomes

## Strong Signals

- Has opinions about information architecture backed by user data
- Can articulate design decisions in terms of business impact
- Experience working within or building design system components
- Shows progression from junior work to more complex problem-solving

## Anti-Patterns

- Portfolio is all visual polish with no process or problem-solving
- Can't explain user research methodology beyond "I talked to users"
- Designs that ignore technical constraints or implementation reality
- Never mentions metrics, conversion, or business outcomes
- Defensive about feedback or can't articulate design rationale

## Interview Focus

- Portfolio walkthrough focusing on process over pixels
- How they handle conflicting stakeholder feedback
- Their approach to validating design assumptions
- Experience working with engineers and understanding technical tradeoffs
