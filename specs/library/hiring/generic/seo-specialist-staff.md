# What We're Looking For

Looking for someone who's moved the needle on organic traffic at scale — not just optimized pages, but architected SEO systems that work across product teams. At staff level, you should have war stories about technical SEO migrations that could have tanked a business, and the judgment to navigate those without breaking things.

This isn't about keyword research and content calendars. We need someone who can look at our technical architecture and see SEO implications that our engineers miss, then build processes so those issues don't happen again. You should be comfortable in Slack channels with backend engineers debugging Core Web Vitals issues and equally comfortable presenting traffic impact to the C-suite.

## Must-Haves

- Owned technical SEO for a site with 1M+ organic monthly visits
- Led at least one major site migration or technical SEO project that could have destroyed organic traffic if done wrong
- Built SEO processes/tooling that scaled across multiple product teams
- Can read and debug JavaScript SEO issues, not just identify them
- Experience influencing engineering priorities without being on the engineering team

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about SEO tooling limitations (knows when to build vs. buy)
- Can articulate the business case for SEO investments in terms of LTV and acquisition cost
- Experience with international/multi-market SEO complexity
- Has mentored other SEO practitioners or trained non-SEO teams on technical SEO

## Anti-Patterns

- SEO experience limited to content sites or e-commerce (SaaS product SEO is different)
- Can't explain technical SEO recommendations to engineers in their language
- Treats SEO as a checklist rather than a system that needs to evolve with the product
- Has never dealt with the political complexity of getting engineering resources for SEO
- Conflates correlation with causation in traffic analysis

## Interview Focus

- Technical migration stories: what could have gone wrong, how they prevented it, what they'd do differently with more resources
- How they've influenced product roadmaps for SEO without being "the person who always says no"
- Their mental model for prioritizing SEO work when everything seems important
- Specific examples of building SEO leverage through process/tooling rather than individual contributor work
