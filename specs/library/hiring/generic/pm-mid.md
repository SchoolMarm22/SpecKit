# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's moved past the "feature factory" phase of product management and can actually think in outcomes. You should have shipped features that failed and learned why — not just collected requirements and threw them over the fence to engineering.

At this level, I expect you to own a problem space, not just a roadmap. You should be able to talk to users without a script, run experiments that actually test hypotheses, and push back on stakeholders when their pet features don't align with user needs. Most importantly, you should understand that engineering constraints aren't obstacles to route around — they're inputs to better product decisions.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped 3+ significant features from conception to post-launch iteration
- Direct user research experience (not just reading research reports)
- Can write technical requirements that engineers don't have to guess about
- Has killed or deprioritized a feature based on data, not politics
- Experience with A/B testing beyond "we tested button colors"

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about what makes good user stories vs. bad ones
- Can explain technical debt tradeoffs in business terms
- Has worked with APIs or understands data models well enough to have intelligent conversations with engineers
- Experience with freemium or B2B pricing decisions

## Anti-Patterns

- Treats engineers like code monkeys who just need better requirements
- Uses metrics like "engagement" without being able to define what engagement actually means for their product
- Has never talked to a user outside of a formal user research session
- Thinks product-market fit is a one-time achievement rather than ongoing work
- Can't explain why their last feature succeeded or failed beyond surface-level metrics

## Interview Focus

- Feature failure stories: what didn't work and how they figured that out
- How they balance user needs vs. business needs vs. technical constraints
- Their process for turning user problems into technical solutions
- How they work with engineering teams during planning and execution
