# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can own content projects from brief to publish without constant oversight. Too many content marketers I've interviewed can write well but can't think strategically about distribution, measurement, or how their work ties to actual business outcomes.

You should understand that content marketing isn't just blogging. It's understanding buyer psychology, knowing which channels actually drive qualified leads for our ICP, and being ruthless about what content to kill when it's not performing. If you've only ever written blog posts that got published and forgotten, this isn't the right fit.

## Must-Haves

- 3+ years creating B2B content that drove measurable pipeline or conversions
- Can write technical content for developer or technical buyer audiences without needing an SME to ghostwrite everything
- Experience with content performance analysis beyond vanity metrics (knows the difference between traffic and qualified traffic)
- Has managed multi-channel content distribution (blog, email, social, community)
- Comfortable with basic HTML/CSS and content management systems

## Strong Signals

- Has A/B tested content formats, headlines, or CTAs and can explain what they learned
- Experience with content attribution and understanding the customer journey
- Can talk intelligently about SEO beyond "I use keywords"
- Has worked with sales teams to create content that actually gets used in deals
- Understanding of technical topics enough to interview engineers or product managers effectively

## Anti-Patterns

- Content marketing experience that was really just social media management
- Can't explain how they measure content success beyond pageviews and shares
- Has never killed underperforming content or campaigns
- Thinks content marketing is just "storytelling" without understanding demand generation
- Only experience is with consumer brands or non-technical B2B

## Interview Focus

- Walk through their content process from ideation to measurement
- How they've handled content that wasn't performing and what they did about it
- Their approach to understanding and writing for technical audiences
- Specific examples of content that drove business outcomes, not just engagement
