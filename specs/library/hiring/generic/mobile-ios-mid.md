# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can own features end-to-end without constant hand-holding. Not necessarily the person designing the system architecture, but definitely someone who can take a spec, ask the right clarifying questions, and ship something users actually want to use.

You should be past the point where every code review is a teaching moment. I want to see evidence that you've made real decisions about iOS patterns, dealt with App Store rejections, and have opinions about things like navigation patterns or state management that come from experience, not just reading blog posts.

We're not looking for someone to just implement designs in a vacuum. Our mobile engineers work directly with designers and product managers, so you need to be comfortable pushing back when something doesn't feel right from a UX or technical perspective.

## Must-Haves

- 2-3 years of production iOS development with apps in the App Store
- Solid understanding of UIKit fundamentals and Auto Layout (SwiftUI is nice but shouldn't be your only UI framework)
- Experience with common iOS patterns: delegates, protocols, closures, and can explain when to use each
- Has dealt with networking, JSON parsing, and asynchronous operations without third-party libraries doing all the work
- Can navigate Xcode profiling tools and has actually used them to solve performance problems

## Strong Signals

- Has shipped features that required integration with platform APIs (camera, location, push notifications, etc.)
- Experience with CoreData or equivalent local storage solutions
- Can articulate opinions about MVVM vs MVC vs VIPER based on actual project experience
- Has worked with REST APIs and can discuss error handling strategies
- Understanding of iOS app lifecycle and memory management

## Anti-Patterns

- Only ever worked on tutorial apps or simple CRUD interfaces
- Relies heavily on third-party libraries for basic functionality without understanding what they do
- Can't explain the difference between strong and weak references or why it matters
- Treats the App Store review process as a black box they just hope works
- Has never had to debug a crash that wasn't immediately obvious from the logs

## Interview Focus

- Code organization: how they structure projects and why they make those choices
- Problem-solving approach when dealing with iOS-specific constraints (memory, battery, network)
- Real examples of technical decisions they've made and owned
- How they approach testing and debugging on actual devices vs simulators
- Understanding of what makes a good mobile UX vs just adapting web patterns
