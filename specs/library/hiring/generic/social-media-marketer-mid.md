# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can run campaigns without me looking over their shoulder. Not junior enough to need constant direction, not senior enough to set strategy, but solidly in that sweet spot where they can take a brief and execute brilliantly.

Most importantly: I want someone who understands that social media marketing at a tech company isn't just posting pretty pictures. You're feeding our funnel, supporting product launches, and representing our brand to developers who can smell inauthentic content from a mile away. The best social marketers I've worked with think like growth marketers who happen to use social channels.

## Must-Haves

- Managed social campaigns that drove measurable business results (leads, signups, revenue — not just engagement)
- Built and executed content calendars across 3+ platforms without constant oversight
- Experience with social advertising on at least two platforms (Meta, LinkedIn, Twitter, TikTok)
- Can analyze performance data and adjust strategy based on what's actually working
- Has worked with technical content or B2B audiences (doesn't have to be tech, but needs to understand complex products)

## Strong Signals

- Grew a company's social following by 50%+ while maintaining engagement rates
- Experience with community management beyond just responding to comments
- Has worked cross-functionally with product or engineering teams
- Comfortable with marketing automation tools (Hootsuite, Sprout Social, Buffer)
- Can write copy that doesn't sound like marketing copy

## Anti-Patterns

- Only experience is B2C lifestyle brands (very different from tech marketing)
- Measures success purely in vanity metrics (likes, follows, impressions)
- Can't explain why they chose one platform over another for a specific campaign
- Treats social media as just a broadcast channel rather than a conversation
- Has never had to justify social spend or tie campaigns to business outcomes

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a campaign from strategy to results — I want to hear their thought process
- How they'd approach social for a technical product they don't understand yet
- Examples of content that performed poorly and what they learned from it
- How they balance brand voice consistency with platform-specific best practices
