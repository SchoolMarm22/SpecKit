# What We're Looking For

I need someone who understands that content marketing isn't just "writing blog posts." At this level, you should be thinking about content as a system — how does a whitepaper connect to a webinar series, which feeds into email nurture campaigns, which supports the sales team's objection handling?

You've probably been burned by vanity metrics before. I want someone who's moved beyond "we got 10k page views" to "we generated 50 qualified leads that sales actually wants to talk to." You should have strong opinions about attribution, lead scoring, and what content actually moves the needle vs. what just makes executives feel good.

This role owns our technical content strategy. You'll be working directly with our engineering team to translate complex product capabilities into stories that prospects actually care about. If you've never had to explain APIs to a non-technical buyer, or figure out how to make developer tools sound compelling to a VP of Engineering, this isn't the role for you.

## Must-Haves

- Built and executed multi-channel content campaigns that generated measurable pipeline (can cite specific revenue attribution)
- Created technical content for B2B audiences — whitepapers, case studies, product documentation, or developer resources
- Managed content operations at scale: editorial calendars, approval workflows, content audits, performance measurement
- Led cross-functional projects with product, sales, and engineering teams to align content with go-to-market strategy
- Experience with marketing automation platforms (HubSpot, Marketo, Pardot) and can set up nurture sequences, not just write emails

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about content distribution that go beyond "post it on LinkedIn"
- Built content that developers or technical buyers actually shared organically
- Experience with ABM or account-specific content creation
- Can explain their content attribution model and defend their channel mix decisions
- Has hired or managed junior content creators

## Anti-Patterns

- "Content marketing" experience that was really just social media management or copywriting
- Can't explain how their content directly contributed to revenue beyond "brand awareness"
- Only worked on consumer content or non-technical B2C campaigns
- Treats content as a creative exercise rather than a growth lever
- Has never worked closely with sales or doesn't understand the handoff between marketing and sales

## Interview Focus

- Content campaign stories: strategy, execution, measurement, what didn't work
- How they approach technical subject matter expertise when they're not the technical expert
- Their philosophy on content quality vs. quantity and how they make those tradeoffs
- Specific examples of collaborating with engineering or product teams to create compelling technical content
- How they think about content ROI and what metrics they actually care about
