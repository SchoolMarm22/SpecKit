# What We're Looking For

I need someone who thinks in systems, not just screens. At this level, you should be defining design strategy across multiple product areas, not just executing on features. You've probably been the design lead who said "we need to stop building one-off solutions" and then actually built the design system that prevented it.

This isn't a senior designer with more years of experience. This is someone who multiplies the effectiveness of other designers, influences product strategy at the executive level, and has strong opinions about how design organizations should work. You should have war stories about technical constraints, cross-functional tensions, and design decisions that affected business metrics in measurable ways.

## Must-Haves

- Led design for a 0-to-1 product that reached meaningful scale (100k+ users)
- Built or significantly evolved a design system used by multiple teams
- Directly influenced product strategy through design research and user insights
- Experience managing through influence across PM, Engineering, and executive stakeholders
- Portfolio showing both strategic thinking and craft execution

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about design process backed by real outcomes
- Can articulate design decisions in business terms, not just user experience terms
- Experience with complex enterprise workflows or multi-sided marketplaces
- Has hired and developed other designers (we're scaling from 4 to 12 designers)
- Comfortable with ambiguous problem spaces and conflicting stakeholder needs

## Anti-Patterns

- Beautiful portfolio but can't explain the business impact of their work
- "User-centered design" philosophy without understanding technical or business constraints
- Only experience at companies where design was already respected and well-resourced
- Focuses on visual polish over systematic thinking
- Can't give concrete examples of changing someone's mind about a design decision

## Interview Focus

- System-level thinking: how they approach design problems that span multiple teams
- Influence without authority: specific examples of changing organizational behavior
- Strategic tradeoffs: times they chose business needs over pure user experience
- Design leadership philosophy: how they develop other designers and elevate design maturity
- Technical fluency: how they work with engineering constraints and opportunities
