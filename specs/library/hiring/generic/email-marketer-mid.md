# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can own email campaigns from strategy to inbox, not just execute on templates someone else built. You should be comfortable setting up automation flows, analyzing performance data, and making strategic recommendations about our lifecycle marketing approach.

The ideal candidate has moved beyond "batch and blast" thinking. You understand that good lifecycle marketing is about behavioral triggers, not calendar scheduling. You've seen what happens when segmentation goes wrong, and you know the difference between vanity metrics and metrics that actually drive business outcomes.

## Must-Haves

- 3+ years managing email campaigns with clear attribution to revenue or retention metrics
- Hands-on experience with marketing automation platforms (Klaviyo, Marketo, HubSpot, etc.)
- Built and optimized multi-step email flows based on user behavior, not just time delays
- Can analyze email performance beyond open rates — understands deliverability, list hygiene, and engagement scoring
- Experience with A/B testing that goes beyond subject lines (send time, frequency, content structure)

## Strong Signals

- Has opinions about email vs. SMS vs. push notifications and when to use each
- Can talk about segmentation strategy that's more sophisticated than demographics
- Experience with cross-channel campaign attribution
- Has managed email deliverability issues (IP warming, domain reputation, etc.)
- Built lifecycle campaigns that span multiple channels

## Anti-Patterns

- Only talks about open rates and click rates without mentioning conversion or revenue
- Can't explain why they chose specific automation triggers
- Has never dealt with deliverability issues (suggests they've never scaled)
- Treats email like a broadcast channel instead of 1:1 communication
- Can't articulate the difference between lifecycle marketing and promotional campaigns

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a lifecycle campaign they built: why those triggers, why that cadence, what would they change
- How they approach segmentation — what data points matter and why
- A time when campaign performance disappointed and how they diagnosed the issue
- Their philosophy on email frequency and how they balance engagement vs. fatigue
