# What We're Looking For

I need someone who can architect our entire marketing measurement strategy, not just run reports. At the staff level, you're not optimizing campaigns — you're building the systems and frameworks that let the entire marketing org optimize themselves.

You should have strong opinions about attribution modeling, incrementality testing, and the death of third-party cookies. More importantly, you should have battle scars from implementing these opinions at scale and living with the consequences. I want someone who's been through at least one major platform migration (Google Analytics 4, iOS 14.5, GDPR) and can tell me what actually broke vs. what everyone said would break.

This role owns our marketing data strategy across 8 different teams and $50M+ in annual spend. You'll be the technical voice in executive conversations about marketing ROI and the strategic voice in engineering conversations about data infrastructure.

## Must-Haves

- Built marketing attribution models beyond last-click (MMM, MTA, or incrementality testing)
- Designed and implemented measurement frameworks for teams spending >$10M annually
- Led cross-functional data projects involving engineering, product, and marketing teams
- Experience with modern marketing data stack (Customer Data Platforms, reverse ETL, identity resolution)
- Can code in Python/R/SQL well enough to build production data pipelines, not just analysis scripts

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about when MMM works vs. when it doesn't (and can defend both sides)
- Experience with privacy-first measurement approaches (first-party data, server-side tracking, cohort analysis)
- Built internal tooling that other analysts actually use
- Has mentored other analysts and can articulate their philosophy on developing analytical talent

## Anti-Patterns

- "Advanced analytics" experience that was really just complex Excel formulas
- Can't explain why their attribution model is better than alternatives
- Treats statistical significance as a binary yes/no rather than understanding confidence intervals
- Has only worked in perfect data environments (no customer journey complexity, no offline touchpoints)
- Focuses on proving marketing works rather than improving how marketing works

## Interview Focus

- Architecture decisions: how they've structured measurement systems and why
- Technical leadership stories: times they've influenced engineering or product decisions through data
- Incrementality philosophy: when they test, how they design tests, what they've learned from failed tests
- How they balance statistical rigor with business speed (perfect attribution vs. good enough attribution)
