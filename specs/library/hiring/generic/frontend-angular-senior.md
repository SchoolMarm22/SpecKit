# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's lived through Angular's evolution — ideally someone who migrated from AngularJS or at least understands why that migration was necessary. The difference between someone who learned Angular 2+ from the start versus someone who lived through the framework wars is substantial.

This isn't about writing components. At the senior level, you should be making architectural decisions that affect the entire frontend codebase. You should have opinions about state management, bundle optimization, and testing strategies that come from real production experience, not just tutorials.

We're looking for someone who can bridge the gap between design and backend teams, who can push back on unrealistic timelines with data, and who can mentor mid-level engineers without becoming a bottleneck.

## Must-Haves

- 4+ years production Angular experience (Angular 2+, not just AngularJS)
- Has architected or significantly refactored a large Angular application (50+ components)
- Deep RxJS experience — can explain when to use switchMap vs mergeMap and why
- Led technical decisions on state management (NgRx, Akita, or custom solutions)
- Experience with Angular testing ecosystem (Jasmine/Karma, Jest, Cypress/Protractor)

## Strong Signals

- Has migrated between major Angular versions in production
- Can articulate specific bundle size optimizations they've implemented
- Experience with micro-frontends or module federation
- Has written custom Angular schematics or libraries
- Strong TypeScript skills beyond basic typing

## Anti-Patterns

- Calls themselves "full-stack" but frontend experience is really just consuming APIs
- Can't explain the difference between OnPush and Default change detection strategies
- Has only worked with Angular CLI defaults without customizing webpack configs
- Dismisses testing as "slowing down development"
- No experience with performance optimization beyond "we use OnPush everywhere"

## Interview Focus

- Architecture decisions: how they've structured large Angular apps and why
- RxJS patterns: real examples of complex async flows they've managed
- Performance war stories: specific bottlenecks they've identified and resolved
- Code review philosophy: what they look for when reviewing junior engineers' code
- Technical leadership: examples of driving consensus on controversial technical decisions
