# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's moved beyond basic keyword research and can actually diagnose why organic traffic dropped 30% last quarter. Too many SEO candidates can recite best practices but fall apart when asked to investigate a real performance issue.

Looking for someone who understands that SEO isn't just about rankings anymore — it's about user intent, technical performance, and how search fits into the broader customer acquisition strategy. You should be comfortable in both Google Analytics and Chrome DevTools.

## Must-Haves

- 3+ years hands-on SEO experience with measurable organic traffic growth
- Can perform technical audits using Screaming Frog, ahrefs, or similar tools
- Experience with JavaScript frameworks and how they impact crawlability
- Has managed content optimization for 50+ pages with clear before/after metrics
- Comfortable working directly with engineers on technical implementations

## Strong Signals

- Built or improved site architecture that resulted in measurable traffic gains
- Experience with Core Web Vitals optimization beyond just knowing what they are
- Can explain their content gap analysis methodology with specific examples
- Has worked with international SEO (hreflang, ccTLD strategies)
- Understanding of how SEO connects to conversion rate and revenue, not just traffic

## Anti-Patterns

- SEO experience limited to blog content and meta tags
- Can't explain why page speed matters beyond "Google said so"
- Thinks link building is still primarily about domain authority
- Has never worked with developers or treats technical SEO as someone else's job
- Focuses only on rankings without connecting to business metrics

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a technical SEO audit they've performed and what actions resulted
- How they approach keyword cannibalization issues on a large site
- Their process for diagnosing organic traffic drops
- How they prioritize SEO improvements when engineering resources are limited
- Real examples of content optimization that moved the needle
