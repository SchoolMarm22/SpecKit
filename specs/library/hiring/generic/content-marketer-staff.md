# What We're Looking For

I need someone who thinks about content as a system, not just individual pieces. At staff level, you're not just writing blog posts — you're architecting our entire content strategy across multiple channels, audiences, and product lines. You should have strong opinions about content attribution, measurement frameworks, and how content ladders up to business outcomes.

Looking for someone who's built content programs from scratch and can show me the data on what worked and what didn't. You should be able to walk into a room of engineers and explain why our developer docs are hurting conversion, then walk into a room of executives and translate content metrics into pipeline impact. This role owns our content voice across the entire customer journey, not just top-of-funnel awareness.

## Must-Haves

- Built and scaled a content program that drove measurable business outcomes (not just vanity metrics)
- Experience with technical content — can write for developers without sounding like marketing wrote it
- Owned content strategy across multiple channels (blog, docs, email, social, video)
- Data-driven approach to content: can build measurement frameworks and optimize based on performance
- Has managed content creators (internal team or external contractors)

## Strong Signals

- Experience with product marketing content (launch narratives, feature announcements, positioning)
- Built content workflows and processes that other teams adopted
- Background in technical writing or developer relations
- Has strong opinions about content attribution and can defend their measurement approach
- Experience with ABM content or account-based strategies

## Anti-Patterns

- "Content marketing" experience that was really just social media management
- Can't connect content metrics to business outcomes beyond "engagement"
- Only experience with B2C or lifestyle brands (B2B SaaS content is different)
- Thinks great content is just about great writing (ignores distribution, timing, audience fit)
- Has never had to justify content budget or ROI to leadership

## Interview Focus

- Content system design: how they'd architect our entire content approach
- Measurement philosophy: what metrics matter and why (the "why" is the signal)
- Cross-functional influence: examples of getting non-marketing teams to adopt their content processes
- Technical depth: can they write content that engineers would actually want to read
- Strategic thinking: how content strategy changes based on company stage, market position, competitive landscape
