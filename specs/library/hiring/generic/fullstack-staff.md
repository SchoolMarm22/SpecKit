# What We're Looking For

I need someone who thinks about systems, not just features. At the staff level, you should be able to look at our entire product engineering org and identify the architectural decisions that are creating friction for teams six months from now.

This isn't about being the best individual contributor—though you need to maintain technical credibility. It's about being the engineer that other senior engineers come to when they're stuck on a gnarly problem that spans multiple services, or when they need to make a decision that will affect how the team works for the next two years. You should have opinions about our technical strategy and be able to articulate why those opinions matter for the business.

## Must-Haves

- Led architectural decisions affecting 3+ teams or services
- Built systems handling 1M+ requests/day with concrete performance trade-offs you can defend
- Mentored senior engineers to staff level (or equivalent career growth)
- Shipped features requiring coordination across backend, frontend, and infrastructure teams
- Made a technical decision you later regretted and can articulate what you learned

## Strong Signals

- Has deprecation stories: successfully sunset legacy systems while keeping products running
- Writes technical specs that other engineers actually want to implement
- Can translate business requirements into technical strategy (not just requirements into code)
- Has strong opinions about API design, data modeling, or frontend architecture backed by real experience
- Built or significantly improved developer tooling that other teams adopted

## Anti-Patterns

- Technical leadership that only works through code review comments
- Can't explain business impact of their architectural decisions
- "Distributed systems" experience that was really just calling microservices
- Always pushes for the newest technology without considering migration costs
- Never worked somewhere that had real scaling problems

## Interview Focus

- System design scenarios that require multi-team coordination
- Times they influenced technical direction without having direct authority
- How they balance technical debt against feature velocity
- Stories about teaching/mentoring that go beyond "I reviewed their PRs"
- What they'd do differently if they were rebuilding our core product today
