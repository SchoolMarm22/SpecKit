# What We're Looking For

I need someone who's moved beyond just implementing features to thinking about the frontend as a system. You should have opinions about state management that go deeper than "Redux vs Context" — you understand when global state is overkill and when component state becomes unmaintainable.

This isn't a role where you'll be heads-down coding 80% of the time. You'll be reviewing PRs, making architectural decisions, and helping other engineers level up. I'm looking for someone who's been burned by their own technical decisions before and learned from it. The best senior engineers I've worked with are the ones who can tell you exactly why they chose one approach over another, including what they gave up.

We ship to millions of users daily, so performance isn't theoretical here — it's revenue impact. Bundle sizes matter. Rendering patterns matter. You should know the difference between what React docs recommend and what actually works at scale.

## Must-Haves

- 5+ years shipping React applications in production with demonstrable impact on user metrics
- Led a major architectural decision (routing, state management, build tooling) and can articulate the tradeoffs
- Experience with performance optimization beyond just running Lighthouse — real profiling, bundle analysis, runtime performance
- Has mentored engineers and helped improve team code quality through tooling or processes
- Strong understanding of modern JavaScript/TypeScript, not just React patterns

## Strong Signals

- Can speak to specific trade-offs between different state management approaches based on real experience
- Has experience with microfrontends or complex multi-app architectures
- Contributions to internal tooling, component libraries, or developer experience improvements
- Experience working closely with design systems and design teams
- Has been on-call for frontend applications and understands observability

## Anti-Patterns

- Only experience with greenfield React apps (we have 4 years of legacy code)
- Can't explain why they chose their state management solution beyond "it's what we used"
- Treats performance as something you optimize after everything else is done
- Views themselves as "just a React developer" rather than a frontend engineer
- Never questions or improves existing patterns, just follows them

## Interview Focus

- Walk through an architectural decision they regret and what they'd do differently
- How they approach performance debugging when users report the app feels "slow"
- Their approach to code review and what they look for beyond syntax errors
- How they balance technical debt against feature velocity
- Specific examples of how they've improved team productivity or code quality
