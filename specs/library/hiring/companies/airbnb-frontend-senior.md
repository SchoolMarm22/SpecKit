# What We're Looking For

We're not just building a booking platform — we're crafting the digital front door to millions of unique spaces where belonging happens. Every interaction a guest has with our product shapes whether they feel confident hitting "Reserve" on a $2,000 week in Tuscany or a $80 night in Brooklyn.

The Guest Experience team owns the most critical user journeys: search, listing pages, booking flow, and trip management. These aren't just CRUD operations — they're trust-building experiences that need to work flawlessly across 220+ countries, in 60+ languages, on every device. We need someone who gets excited about the intersection of technical excellence and human psychology, because a 100ms delay in search can mean the difference between a guest finding their perfect trip or bouncing to a competitor.

Our design system (DLS) is mature and opinionated, our React components are battle-tested at scale, but we're constantly evolving. The person in this role will help shape how 50+ million guests interact with Airbnb every quarter.

## Must-Haves

- 5+ years shipping React at consumer scale with real performance constraints (not internal tools or dashboards)
- Experience with internationalization and accessibility as first-class concerns, not afterthoughts
- Has owned a user journey end-to-end: from understanding user research to measuring conversion impact post-launch
- Can articulate technical decisions through a business impact lens — knows when to optimize for speed vs. when to optimize for maintainability
- Experience with design systems at scale: both consuming and contributing to component libraries

## Strong Signals

- Has worked closely with designers and can speak their language (understands design specs, tokens, component states)
- Experience with A/B testing frameworks and knows how to build experiments that don't break
- Has optimized web vitals on a site that actually matters (Core Web Vitals scores affect our SEO rankings)
- Travel industry or marketplace experience (understands trust, safety, high-consideration purchases)
- Can debug across the stack when their frontend hits backend issues

## Anti-Patterns

- "Pixel perfect" obsession without understanding responsive design principles or mobile-first thinking
- Only worked on B2B products (consumer expectations for polish and performance are different)
- Treats accessibility as a checkbox exercise rather than understanding it as inclusive design
- Can't explain why they made performance optimizations beyond "the bundle was big"
- Dismissive of design feedback or treats designers as "just making things pretty"

## Interview Focus

- Guest empathy: How do they think about edge cases that affect real people's travel plans?
- Craft quality: Can they explain technical decisions through the lens of user experience?
- Collaboration depth: How have they worked with designers, PMs, and data scientists on ambiguous problems?
- Scale understanding: What happens to their React component when it needs to work in 60 languages?
- Business impact thinking: How do they balance technical debt against shipping features that move conversion?
