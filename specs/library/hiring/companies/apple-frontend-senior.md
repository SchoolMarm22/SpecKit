# What We're Looking For

Apple Music isn't just another streaming app. We're building the definitive music experience that lives at the intersection of hardware and software — from AirPods spatial audio to CarPlay integration to the way lyrics sync with the beat. This requires engineers who think about craft first, optimization second, and shipping third.

You'll be the DRI for significant portions of our web experience, working with a small team that ships to hundreds of millions of users. We don't move fast and break things here. We move thoughtfully and ship experiences that feel inevitable once users touch them. If you've only worked at places where "good enough" ships on Friday, this isn't the role for you.

Our interview process is thorough because the work demands it. Expect multiple rounds with deep technical discussions, live coding that goes beyond algorithms, and conversations about how you think about user experience at Apple scale.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped production web applications handling millions of concurrent users with measurable performance impact
- Deep understanding of Web Audio API, media streaming, or complex audio/video workflows (we're not building todo apps)
- Experience optimizing for Safari specifically — not just "cross-browser" but understanding WebKit internals and Safari's unique behaviors
- Track record as a DRI: owned a feature end-to-end where you made the final technical decisions and lived with the consequences
- Can articulate strong opinions about web performance that go beyond "bundle size bad"

## Strong Signals

- Has worked on media applications or real-time audio/video experiences
- Experience with accessibility at scale — not just aria-labels but building for users with diverse needs across our entire ecosystem
- Understanding of how web applications integrate with native mobile apps or desktop applications
- Has made architectural decisions they later had to live with for years, not quarters

## Anti-Patterns

- Résumé shows pattern of shipping MVPs and moving to the next company before seeing long-term impact
- Can't explain the difference between technical debt and technical investment
- Views design feedback as "nitpicking" rather than understanding that pixel-perfect execution is table stakes
- Experience only in fast-moving startup environments where "we'll fix it later" is acceptable
- Treats web standards as suggestions rather than foundations to build upon

## Interview Focus

- Technical deep-dive on a complex media feature they've built — we want to understand their thinking process, not just the outcome
- Live coding session focused on real problems: audio visualization, playlist management, or streaming optimization
- System design conversation about building music discovery features that work across web, mobile, and embedded devices
- Discussion of how they handle ambiguous requirements and technical tradeoffs when there's no "right" answer
