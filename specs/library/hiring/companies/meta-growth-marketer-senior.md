# What We're Looking For

At Meta, growth marketing isn't about running Facebook ads or writing blog posts. You'll be working directly with engineers, designing experiments that touch billions of users, and making product decisions based on statistical significance, not gut feelings.

Our growth team ships code. Not metaphorically — you'll have commits in our repos. We don't hand specs to engineers; we pair with them on implementation. If you've never opened a pull request or can't read a SQL query, this isn't the role for you. We move fast because we understand the technical constraints, not despite them.

The scale changes everything. A 0.1% improvement in signup conversion isn't just a nice metric — it's tens of millions of new users. Your experiments will run on infrastructure that processes petabytes of data daily, and you'll need to think about statistical power when your sample sizes make academic papers look quaint.

## Must-Haves

- Designed and shipped A/B tests at >1M user scale with proper statistical methodology
- Can write SQL for complex user behavior analysis (CTEs, window functions, not just SELECT/WHERE)
- Experience with experimentation platforms (our internal tools or external ones like Optimizely/LaunchDarkly)
- Collaborated directly with engineers on growth feature implementation
- Understands funnel analysis, cohort retention, and can spot Simpson's paradox

## Strong Signals

- Has shipping experience with growth features (onboarding flows, viral mechanics, notification systems)
- Can read basic Python/JavaScript to understand experiment implementation
- Experience with machine learning applications in growth (recommendation systems, predictive modeling)
- Has worked on technical growth problems (performance optimization affecting conversion, mobile vs. web behavior)

## Anti-Patterns

- "Growth hacking" mindset focused on tricks rather than sustainable systems
- Can't distinguish between correlation and causation in their own case studies
- Treats data science as a black box (just gives requirements, doesn't understand output)
- Only worked on acquisition, never retention or activation
- Dismisses technical constraints as "engineering problems to solve later"

## Interview Focus

- Walk through an experiment they designed: hypothesis, implementation, statistical approach, and what they learned from negative results
- Technical fluency: can they spot the flaw in a poorly designed experiment or explain why our internal experimentation platform makes certain architectural choices
- Scale thinking: how their approach would change going from 100k to 100M users
- Cross-functional collaboration: specific examples of working with engineers on growth infrastructure
