# What We're Looking For

We're not looking for a typical consumer PM who thinks in A/B tests and conversion funnels. Search PM at Google means understanding information retrieval at planetary scale — how query understanding affects 8 billion searches daily, why latency matters at the 99th percentile, and how ranking changes ripple through the entire web ecosystem.

You'll be working with Research scientists who publish at top-tier conferences, SWEs who think about cache hierarchies across 20+ datacenters, and UXRs who run studies with statistical power most companies can't dream of. This isn't about growth hacking or engagement metrics — it's about fundamental improvements to how humans access information.

The promo culture here means your design docs will be read by Principal Engineers and Distinguished Scientists. Your technical depth needs to match the room you're walking into.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped ML-driven products at scale (millions+ users) with measurable quality improvements
- Written design docs that influenced architecture decisions across multiple teams
- Deep understanding of search ranking, NLP, or recommendation systems — not just "worked with ML team"
- Experience with gradual rollouts and statistical significance testing at large scale
- Can hold technical conversations about precision/recall tradeoffs, embedding spaces, or distributed systems bottlenecks

## Strong Signals

- Published research or patents in relevant domains (IR, ML, HCI)
- Led cross-functional initiatives spanning 5+ engineering teams
- Experience with abuse/spam mitigation or content quality at scale
- Has navigated technical disagreements with Research teams and found solutions that satisfy both product and research constraints

## Anti-Patterns

- "Data-driven" means they can read a dashboard but can't design an experiment
- Thinks PM means writing requirements for engineers to implement
- Can't distinguish between correlation and causation in their own launch metrics
- Views technical debt as "engineering's problem"
- Measures success only in engagement metrics without understanding quality implications

## Interview Focus

- Technical depth: can they explain why their ranking improvement worked?
- Systems thinking: how do they reason about emergent behaviors in complex systems?
- Research collaboration: how do they translate between product needs and research innovation?
- Scale intuition: do they understand the difference between 10M and 1B user problems?
- Design doc quality: can they write technical specifications that engineers actually want to implement?
