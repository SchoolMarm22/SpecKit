# What We're Looking For

You'll be joining a team that keeps Google Cloud running for millions of developers worldwide. This isn't about keeping a single service up — it's about maintaining the reliability of infrastructure that other teams depend on to build their own reliable systems.

We expect you to think in design docs, not just tickets. Every meaningful change starts with a doc that gets reviewed by senior engineers across the org. Your proposals will be scrutinized by people who've been doing this longer than most companies have existed. That's the standard, and it makes our systems better.

This role is on the strong IC track. L5 SREs here own entire reliability domains — you might be the person responsible for ensuring our container orchestration scales to handle Black Friday traffic spikes, or the one who designs our approach to cross-region failover. Promo to L6 means you've driven projects that measurably improved reliability for hundreds of services.

## Must-Haves

- Operated distributed systems at serious scale (think millions of QPS, not thousands)
- Written design docs that influenced cross-team technical decisions
- Built monitoring and alerting systems that other engineers actually trust
- Experience with at least one major incident where you were the point person coordinating the response
- Comfortable with our stack: Borg, Spanner, Bigtable, or equivalent distributed systems

## Strong Signals

- Has deleted code that was "temporarily" added 3 years ago and somehow became load-bearing
- Can articulate why SLOs matter more than SLAs and has examples of SLO-driven decision making
- Experience with capacity planning that involves actual math, not just "let's add more machines"
- Has built tools that other SREs use (bonus if they're still using them after you left that team)

## Anti-Patterns

- Thinks "DevOps" and "SRE" are the same thing
- Can't explain why they chose specific percentiles for their SLIs
- Has never pushed back on a feature request because of reliability concerns
- Treats toil reduction as someone else's problem
- Confuses high availability with disaster recovery

## Interview Focus

- System design at Google scale — we'll ask about real problems we face
- Past design docs: can you walk us through one that changed how your team operated?
- Incident response: not just what happened, but how you prioritized competing urgent demands
- Measurement philosophy: how do you know if a system is actually reliable?
- Code review scenarios using our actual review tool and standards
