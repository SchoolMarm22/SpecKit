# What We're Looking For

You'll join a team that moves at Meta speed, which means your code needs to work for 3 billion people from day one. We're not looking for someone who can build features — we need someone who can build features that scale to populations larger than most countries.

Our bootcamp will get you up to speed on our stack, but the real test is how quickly you can identify high-impact problems and ship solutions. We have engineers who joined as new grads and shipped features used by hundreds of millions of people within their first year. The difference isn't years of experience — it's judgment about what matters.

We care more about your ability to reason through performance at scale than your familiarity with our specific tools. React is our framework, but we've modified it so heavily for our needs that prior React experience is less important than understanding why React made certain design choices in the first place.

## Must-Haves

- Built React applications handling significant scale (millions of users, not thousands)
- Can articulate performance bottlenecks in frontend apps beyond "it's slow" — understands bundle size, rendering performance, memory leaks
- Has shipped features where a bug would affect real user behavior, not just internal tools
- Demonstrates strong product intuition — can push back on designs or requirements when they don't serve users
- Experience with A/B testing or gradual rollouts — understands that "working" and "working for everyone" are different problems

## Strong Signals

- Open source contributions to libraries, not just projects (shows they think about API design for other developers)
- Has worked on a feature that was killed after shipping and can explain what they learned
- Experience with internal tooling or developer productivity — our internal tools are products too
- Can explain a time they optimized for developer velocity over code perfection (or vice versa)

## Anti-Patterns

- Treats "best practices" as gospel without understanding the tradeoffs
- Can't explain why they chose their current tech stack beyond "it's what we used"
- Has only worked on features that took months to ship — we need people who can deliver impact in weeks
- Focuses on code elegance over user impact
- Dismisses performance concerns as premature optimization

## Interview Focus

- System design at frontend scale — how do you handle state management when your app has hundreds of components?
- Impact measurement — how do you know if your feature is successful?
- Tradeoff decisions — when do you ship the 80% solution vs. building it right?
- Technical ownership stories — times they drove technical decisions that affected multiple teams
- Performance war stories — specific optimizations they've made and how they measured success
