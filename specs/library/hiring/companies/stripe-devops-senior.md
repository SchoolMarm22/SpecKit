# What We're Looking For

At Stripe, infrastructure is a product with developers as users. You'll be building platforms that thousands of engineers rely on daily, which means your APIs need to be as thoughtfully designed as our external ones. We're not looking for someone who just keeps the lights on — we need an engineer who thinks in primitives and abstractions.

Our infrastructure team operates like a product team. You'll write memos justifying architectural decisions, not just implement tickets. You'll be on-call for services you build because we believe ownership includes operational responsibility. The bar for code quality is identical whether you're shipping user-facing features or internal tooling.

We value long-term thinking over quick fixes. The platform decisions you make today will compound over years as we scale from thousands to tens of thousands of engineers. This is both the opportunity and the responsibility.

## Must-Haves

- Built developer platforms or tools used by 100+ engineers daily
- Designed APIs for infrastructure services (not just REST endpoints — think about ergonomics, composability, error handling)
- Operational experience with multi-region distributed systems at meaningful scale
- Can write production-quality code in at least one systems language (Go, Rust, or equivalent)
- Experience with infrastructure as code where you owned the abstractions, not just wrote Terraform

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about developer experience backed by concrete examples
- Writes technical documentation that other engineers actually want to read
- Experience building or significantly improving CI/CD systems with thoughtful abstractions
- Can articulate infrastructure reliability principles beyond "five nines good"
- Has been on-call for systems they built and can discuss incident response philosophy

## Anti-Patterns

- Views infrastructure as separate from product engineering — we're all building products here
- Can't explain why their infrastructure APIs are shaped the way they are
- Treats monitoring as an afterthought rather than a core product requirement
- "Platform engineer" experience that was really just Kubernetes YAML without designing abstractions
- Dismisses the importance of API ergonomics because "it's just internal tooling"

## Interview Focus

- API design philosophy: how they think about abstractions, backward compatibility, error surfaces
- Memo-quality technical communication — can they write design docs that influence decisions?
- Systems thinking: how they approach reliability, observability, and operational simplicity
- Product mindset: how they prioritize developer experience alongside technical requirements
- Ownership mentality: examples of taking responsibility for the full lifecycle of their systems
