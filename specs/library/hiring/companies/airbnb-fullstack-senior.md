# What We're Looking For

We're building the platform that helps millions of hosts create magical experiences for guests worldwide. This isn't just about building features — it's about understanding that behind every booking is a host who's opening their home and a guest looking for belonging.

Our Host Platform team sits at the intersection of complex marketplace dynamics, financial systems, and human psychology. You'll work on everything from pricing algorithms that help hosts optimize their earnings to onboarding flows that turn nervous first-time hosts into confident entrepreneurs. We ship to production multiple times a day using our service-oriented architecture, and every change you make impacts real people's livelihoods.

Post-2020, we rebuilt leaner but stronger. Brian still reviews designs personally. Quality of craft isn't negotiable — we'd rather ship one thing excellently than three things adequately. Our design system (built on React) is world-class, and you'll be expected to use it thoughtfully, not just copy-paste components.

## Must-Haves

- 4+ years full-stack experience with React and either Rails, Node.js, or Java (we're primarily Rails/React)
- Built and maintained features in a service-oriented architecture with 20+ services
- Experience with marketplace or two-sided platform dynamics (hosts vs guests, buyers vs sellers, etc.)
- Shipped A/B tests at scale and can articulate the difference between statistical significance and business impact
- Strong CSS skills — can implement pixel-perfect designs without breaking responsive behavior

## Strong Signals

- Hospitality, travel, or marketplace experience (understands the domain complexity)
- Has worked closely with designers and can speak to design rationale, not just implementation
- Experience with financial systems or payment flows (PCI compliance, reconciliation, etc.)
- Contributed to or built design systems from scratch
- Previous experience at companies that went through major pivots or layoffs

## Anti-Patterns

- Can't explain how their work connected to business metrics beyond "increased engagement"
- Treats design as a pixel-pushing exercise rather than problem-solving
- Only comfortable with greenfield development (our codebase has history)
- Talks about users as abstract entities rather than real people with real needs
- Dismissive of accessibility or internationalization as "edge cases"

## Interview Focus

- Host empathy: can they think through the host journey and identify pain points?
- Design partnership: how do they collaborate with designers during ambiguous problem spaces?
- System thinking: how do they approach building features that work across our service architecture?
- Quality mindset: examples of when they pushed back on shipping something that wasn't ready
- Marketplace intuition: understanding of two-sided platform dynamics and network effects
