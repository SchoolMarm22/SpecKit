# What We're Looking For

We're building the storefront experience that millions of merchants rely on to run their businesses. This isn't just another React job — you'll be working on code that directly impacts whether a small business in Kansas can pay their rent this month.

Our Storefront team owns everything from the checkout flow to the product pages that convert browsers into buyers. We're dealing with Rails at a scale most engineers will never see (seriously, our monolith makes other "large" Rails apps look tiny), and we need someone who can think in systems while still caring about the 50ms that makes the difference between a sale and an abandoned cart. You'll be working with Polaris, but more importantly, you'll be extending it and sometimes breaking its rules when merchant needs demand it.

We're Digital by Default, which means you'll be collaborating with designers in Figma, pairing with backend engineers on Rails controllers, and shipping features that get measured in real revenue impact, not vanity metrics.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped production React at e-commerce scale (conversion rates matter, not just uptime)
- Experience with Rails or equivalent MVC frameworks — you'll be working closely with our Rails controllers and need to understand the request lifecycle
- Has optimized for real user metrics (Core Web Vitals, conversion rates) not just synthetic benchmarks
- Can read and contribute to large codebases — our Storefront codebase has history and opinions baked in
- Comfortable with A/B testing frontend changes that affect real business metrics

## Strong Signals

- Has worked on checkout or payment flows (understands the UX complexity of money changing hands)
- Experience with design systems at scale — bonus if you've contributed back to the system, not just consumed it
- Has dealt with international/multi-currency frontend challenges
- Open source contributions to tools merchants actually use
- Experience with CLI tooling or developer experience improvements

## Anti-Patterns

- Frontend experience that was purely internal tools (merchant-facing is different)
- Can't explain the performance impact of their React patterns in real terms
- Treats design systems as rigid rules rather than merchant-focused guidelines
- Has never had to optimize for slow networks or older devices (our merchants' customers aren't all on fiber)
- Only worked on greenfield projects (our storefront has evolved over 15+ years)

## Interview Focus

- Merchant empathy: can they think about features from a small business owner's perspective
- System thinking: how do they approach performance when serving millions of storefronts
- Craft vs. speed tradeoffs: when do you refactor vs. when do you ship
- Real examples of frontend work that moved business metrics, not just technical metrics
