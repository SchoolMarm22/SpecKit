# What We're Looking For

Our users are developers, which means they have no patience for interfaces that feel like afterthoughts. They'll abandon a poorly-designed API explorer in seconds and write curl commands instead. They expect our developer tools to feel as polished as our payments flow.

At Stripe, developer experience isn't a nice-to-have — it's how we compete. Our Dashboard, CLI, docs, and SDKs are products that developers choose to use, not enterprise software they're forced to adopt. This means every interaction, from first authentication to webhook debugging, needs to feel inevitable.

We're looking for a designer who understands that developer tools are still tools, and tools should disappear into the workflow. Someone who can design complex technical interfaces that feel simple, and simple interfaces that handle complex edge cases gracefully.

## Must-Haves

- Designed developer-facing products (APIs, dashboards, CLIs, or dev tools) that real developers use daily
- Can read code well enough to understand what they're designing for — comfort with REST APIs, webhooks, rate limiting concepts
- Portfolio showing complex information architecture work, not just pretty interfaces
- Experience designing for power users who know exactly what they want and hate extra clicks
- Has worked closely with engineers on implementation — understands CSS limitations and what's expensive to build

## Strong Signals

- Has used Stripe's products extensively and has informed opinions about what works and what doesn't
- Experience with technical documentation or developer education
- Understands the difference between designing for consumer users vs. developers (hint: developers read)
- Can articulate design decisions in writing, not just in presentations
- Has designed error states that actually help users debug problems

## Anti-Patterns

- Portfolio full of consumer apps but no B2B or technical products
- Can't explain technical constraints or talks about engineering as "implementation details"
- Designs that look beautiful in Figma but would break with real data volumes
- Never worked on products where the user's job depends on the interface working correctly
- Thinks developers don't care about good design (they care more than anyone)

## Interview Focus

- API design philosophy: how should complexity be layered in developer interfaces?
- Error handling: show examples of technical error states you've designed and why
- Workflow thinking: how do you design for users who are trying to accomplish technical tasks under pressure?
- Stripe product critique: what would you change about our Dashboard and why?
