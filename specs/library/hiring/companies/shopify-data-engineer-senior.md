# What We're Looking For

We're not just moving data around — we're building the analytics foundation that helps millions of merchants understand their businesses. When a merchant in Bangkok needs to know why their conversion dropped last Tuesday, or when a Plus merchant wants to segment customers by lifetime value, our pipelines better work flawlessly.

At Shopify scale (millions of merchants, billions in GMV), data engineering isn't about clever algorithms. It's about building systems that are reliable enough to bet a business on, fast enough to power real-time decisions, and clear enough that a merchant can actually understand their own data. We optimize for merchant impact over engineering elegance, but we do it with craft.

You'll be working closely with our Product Analytics and Business Intelligence teams, but also directly with merchant-facing product teams. This isn't a role where you throw data over the wall — you'll see how merchants actually use what you build.

## Must-Haves

- Built and maintained production data pipelines processing >10M events/day with demonstrable uptime (not "big data" toy projects)
- Hands-on SQL optimization experience — can explain query plans, has opinions about indexing strategies, knows when to denormalize
- Experience with event streaming architecture (Kafka, Kinesis, or similar) in a high-throughput production environment
- Has debugged data quality issues in production and implemented systematic solutions (not just one-off fixes)
- Can code in Python or Ruby well enough to contribute to our existing data platform (we're largely Python for data, but Ruby knowledge helps with Rails integration)

## Strong Signals

- E-commerce or marketplace analytics experience (understands concepts like cohort analysis, funnel optimization, merchant segmentation)
- Has built self-service analytics tools that non-technical users actually used
- Experience with dimensional modeling and can articulate when to use star schema vs. wide tables
- Contributed to open source data tools or has strong opinions about the modern data stack
- Has worked in a high-growth environment where data requirements changed monthly

## Anti-Patterns

- Only worked with batch processing — real-time merchant needs require streaming mindset
- Treats data quality as someone else's problem or thinks "garbage in, garbage out" is acceptable
- Academic big data experience without production operational responsibility
- Can't explain their pipeline design choices in terms of business impact
- Dismisses "simple" solutions in favor of over-engineered distributed systems

## Interview Focus

- Data modeling decisions: how they structure analytics for business users who aren't data scientists
- Pipeline reliability: specific examples of how they've handled data quality issues or system failures
- Merchant empathy: can they think through what analytics a small business owner actually needs vs. what's technically interesting
- Scale challenges: how they've evolved systems as data volume grew, what broke and how they fixed it
