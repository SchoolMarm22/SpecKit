# What We're Looking For

We're building design tools for designers, which means our users are us. They know exactly what good feels like because they live in our product 8 hours a day. There's no hiding behind user research when your users can articulate the 2-pixel difference that makes a feature feel broken.

The Core Editor is where the magic happens — canvas performance, selection behaviors, the stuff that makes Figma feel like native software in a browser. We're not looking for someone who can make things look pretty. We need someone who understands that in design tools, the interaction IS the interface. You need to think in 60fps and design for multiplayer from day one.

Our design-engineering partnership isn't ceremonial. Engineers don't just "implement designs" — we're building the same product together. You'll be in the WebGL performance reviews, the WASM memory discussions, the debates about whether a feature is technically feasible. If you've never had to explain to an engineer why 16.67ms matters for a hover state, this isn't the role.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped interaction-heavy products where performance was a constraint (not just "made it responsive")
- Experience designing developer tools, creative tools, or other professional software (consumer social doesn't translate)
- Can articulate the difference between designing for creation vs. consumption workflows
- Deep fluency with Figma as a power user — you should know our keyboard shortcuts better than we do
- Has worked closely with engineers on implementation, not just "thrown designs over the wall"

## Strong Signals

- Built or contributed to design systems at scale (complex component APIs, not just color tokens)
- Experience with real-time collaborative interfaces or multiplayer product challenges
- Understanding of technical constraints like browser rendering, canvas performance, or memory management
- Has strong opinions about design tooling that come from actually using the tools daily

## Anti-Patterns

- "Product design" experience that was really just UI design with user research sprinkled on top
- Can't explain their design decisions beyond "it tests well" or "users like it"
- Treats engineering constraints as problems to solve rather than creative parameters to design within
- Portfolio heavy on marketing sites, light on complex interaction patterns
- Has never been in a technical architecture discussion

## Interview Focus

- Canvas interaction philosophy: how should selection work in a multiplayer canvas? What about conflicting actions?
- Technical partnership stories: specific examples of working through implementation constraints with engineers
- Performance intuition: when does visual polish conflict with 60fps? How do you decide?
- Tool-building philosophy: what makes a good creative tool different from good consumer software?
