# What We're Looking For

The Shop app isn't just another e-commerce mobile app — it's the merchant's lifeline when they're away from their computer. I've seen merchants frantically trying to fulfill orders from their phone at their kid's soccer game, and our app better not let them down.

We need someone who gets that mobile-first means merchant-first. The person who joins us will own features that directly impact how millions of merchants run their businesses. You'll be working with our Rails API (one of the largest in the world), but you need to think mobile-native, not "web views wrapped in an app."

We're remote-first but high-collaboration. Expect to pair with backend engineers in Toronto, designers in Vancouver, and merchants literally everywhere. If you're the type who needs someone looking over your shoulder to ship, this isn't the role.

## Must-Haves

- Native iOS or Android experience with apps that have >1M users in production
- Has built complex offline-first mobile experiences (merchants need to work without wifi)
- GraphQL experience at scale — our mobile API serves 100k+ requests per minute
- Can articulate the performance tradeoffs of different mobile architectures (we obsess over app startup time)
- Merchant empathy: has worked on B2B mobile apps or can clearly explain why consumer mobile patterns don't work for business users

## Strong Signals

- Contributed to mobile developer tooling or built internal CLI tools
- Experience with React Native or Flutter (we're exploring cross-platform)
- Has shipped features during peak traffic events (Black Friday energy)
- Can explain mobile-specific API design patterns beyond REST
- Previous remote work at a product company (agency experience doesn't translate)

## Anti-Patterns

- Only consumer mobile app experience — B2B mobile is fundamentally different UX
- Thinks mobile is just "smaller screens" rather than a different interaction paradigm
- Can't explain why they chose their current mobile architecture
- Has never been on-call for a mobile app in production
- Dismisses "boring" features like push notifications as simple (they're not)

## Interview Focus

- Walk through a mobile feature they owned end-to-end, including the API design conversations
- How they'd architect offline sync for merchant inventory updates
- Debugging mobile performance issues under production load
- What they'd build for merchants if given a month of Hack Days
- How they stay close to merchant needs when building mobile experiences
