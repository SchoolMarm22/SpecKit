# What We're Looking For

I need someone who gets energized by talking to merchants directly, not someone who treats merchants like personas in a deck. At Shopify, merchant obsession isn't a buzzword — it's the filter for every product decision. You should have stories about times you shipped something because a merchant told you it would save them 10 minutes a day, even if the engineering effort seemed disproportionate.

This role owns the merchant onboarding and setup experience, which touches everything from our Rails admin to the React-based setup flows. You don't need to code, but you need to understand why our engineers get excited about optimizing Rails queries and why our design system (Polaris) exists. When engineering says "that's a breaking change to the GraphQL schema," you should know what that means for rollout strategy.

We're remote-first but relationship-heavy. Your 1:1s with engineering leads matter more than your roadmaps. During Hack Days, you should be the PM who knows which merchant pain points are worth the team's 48 hours, not the one asking what everyone's building.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped B2B products where your users' revenue depended on what you built (SaaS, marketplace, or e-commerce tools)
- Can explain a time you changed product direction based on direct user feedback, not analytics
- Has worked with engineering teams on API design or schema changes (you don't write code, but you understand the constraints)
- Experience with multi-sided marketplace dynamics (merchants vs. customers, platform vs. third-party developers)

## Strong Signals

- Has run usability sessions or merchant interviews themselves, not just consumed research
- Understands technical concepts like rate limiting, webhooks, or API versioning well enough to make product tradeoffs
- Can tell a story about advocating for a less elegant solution because it solved a real merchant problem faster
- Experience with international or multi-currency products (our merchants are global)
- Has worked at a company that grew from hundreds to thousands of customers while they were there

## Anti-Patterns

- Treats engineering estimates as the start of a negotiation rather than information to inform scope decisions
- Can't name a specific merchant or customer whose business they understand deeply
- Uses "user journey" and "user experience" but has never actually watched someone use their product
- Thinks product strategy means roadmaps and OKRs rather than opinionated bets about merchant behavior
- Has only worked on consumer products or internal tools (B2B merchants are different, and our merchants are trying to run businesses)

## Interview Focus

- Merchant stories: Can they tell specific stories about merchants they've learned from? Not "users said" but "Sarah from the pottery shop told me..."
- Technical collaboration: How do they work with engineers when requirements conflict with technical constraints? Do they push back or find creative solutions?
- International complexity: How would they think about rolling out a feature to merchants in 175+ countries with different tax laws, languages, and payment methods?
- Craft vs. speed tradeoffs: Can they articulate when to ship fast and messy vs. when merchant trust requires getting it right the first time?
