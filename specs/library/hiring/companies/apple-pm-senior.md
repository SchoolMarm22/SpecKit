# What We're Looking For

This isn't a typical PM role where you write PRDs and run standups. You'll be the DRI for experiences that span silicon, firmware, operating systems, and applications. When we ship, millions of people will touch what you built every day, and they'll notice if it's not right.

You need to think like an engineer but advocate like a user. Our hardware teams are building chips three years before software will run on them. Our software teams are optimizing for hardware that doesn't exist yet. You're the person who sees both sides and makes the impossible timeline work.

The hardest part isn't managing up or managing out — it's that you can't talk about what you're building, even with other teams inside Apple. You'll make decisions with incomplete information and defend them without revealing context. This requires a different kind of conviction.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped hardware-software integrated products where you owned the complete user experience (not just the app layer)
- Collaborated with silicon teams on architecture decisions that affected software 2+ years later
- Managed product development where revealing timeline or feature details externally would create competitive risk
- Experience making irreversible technical bets with 6-figure+ financial implications
- Can debug cross-stack issues from user behavior down to hardware constraints

## Strong Signals

- Has killed a feature they championed after discovering it degraded the core experience
- Experience with thermal, power, or performance constraints that shaped product decisions
- Can articulate why certain technical tradeoffs were right for the product but wrong for the market
- Has worked in environments where "fast follow" isn't an option

## Anti-Patterns

- PM experience limited to pure software products or services
- Needs large teams or external validation to make decisions
- Thinks "minimum viable" is compatible with consumer hardware
- Can't explain technical constraints in business terms or business constraints in technical terms
- Treats hardware timelines as moveable or negotiable

## Interview Focus

- Cross-functional influence: How do you align teams that can't share context with each other?
- Technical depth: Can you debug a performance regression that spans three different engineering teams?
- Conviction under uncertainty: Walk through a bet you made with incomplete information
- Quality philosophy: When do you ship versus when do you wait another cycle?
