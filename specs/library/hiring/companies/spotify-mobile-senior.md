# What We're Looking For

We're not just building another media app — we're building the world's audio platform. Our mobile engineers need to understand that every millisecond of latency affects whether someone discovers their new favorite artist. When you're handling 500M+ users across wildly different network conditions and device capabilities, "it works on my phone" isn't good enough.

Looking for someone who's lived through the complexity of offline-first architecture with real-time sync. Music apps are different — users expect their playlists to work in airplane mode, but also expect live collaborative features. You'll be working in a squad that owns a specific domain (could be discovery, player, social, creator tools) with full autonomy over technical decisions, but you need to play well with 100+ other squads shipping independently.

Our mobile codebase has evolved over 15+ years. You'll inherit technical debt that made sense in 2010 but needs careful refactoring. We value engineers who can balance shipping features that move metrics with the long-term health of the platform.

## Must-Haves

- 5+ years mobile development with at least one complex media or audio app in production
- Experience with offline-first architecture and data synchronization strategies
- Shipped features that were A/B tested and can talk about what the data taught them
- Has worked on apps with significant background processing requirements (audio playback, downloads, sync)
- Can articulate the tradeoffs between native performance and cross-platform solutions

## Strong Signals

- Audio engineering background or deep understanding of media streaming protocols
- Experience with large-scale mobile apps (1M+ users) and the performance challenges that brings
- Has built or contributed to design systems or shared mobile component libraries
- Understanding of mobile analytics and crash reporting beyond just "we use Firebase"
- Previous experience at companies with autonomous team structures

## Anti-Patterns

- Only worked on simple CRUD apps without complex state management
- Can't explain their approach to mobile architecture beyond following platform conventions
- Never dealt with backward compatibility across multiple app versions in the wild
- Treats mobile as just a thin client for backend APIs without understanding mobile-specific constraints
- Dismissive of cross-platform approaches or overly religious about native-only solutions

## Interview Focus

- Audio/media streaming challenges: How would you handle offline playback with remote queue updates?
- Squad autonomy: How do you make architectural decisions when 10 other squads depend on your APIs?
- Performance at scale: Walk through debugging a memory leak affecting 1% of users on older Android devices
- Data-driven product decisions: How do you instrument features to learn from user behavior?
- Legacy evolution: How do you refactor a critical user flow without breaking existing functionality?
