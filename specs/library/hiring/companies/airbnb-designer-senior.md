# What We're Looking For

Belonging isn't just our mission statement — it's the lens through which every design decision gets made. At Airbnb, design isn't a service organization that makes engineers' work pretty. We're a design-driven company where the design craft drives product strategy, and our designers sit at the table with PMs and engineers as true product partners.

You'll be working on Core Experience, which means your work directly impacts the first impression millions of people have of Airbnb. Brian is deeply involved in craft reviews, so your work will be scrutinized by someone who cares about every pixel. This isn't about ego — it's about the fact that design quality is directly correlated with trust, and trust is everything in our two-sided marketplace.

Post-2020, we rebuilt leaner and more focused. We can't afford design debt or "we'll polish it later" thinking. Everything ships at craft quality, and every designer here knows how to work within systems while still pushing them forward.

## Must-Haves

- 5+ years designing complex, multi-stakeholder products (two-sided marketplaces preferred)
- Portfolio showing systematic thinking — how individual features connect to broader user journeys
- Experience working within and evolving design systems at scale (you'll be working with our DLS extensively)
- Demonstrated ability to design for trust and safety (our users are literally staying in strangers' homes)
- Strong craft fundamentals: typography, visual hierarchy, interaction design that feels native to mobile and web

## Strong Signals

- Has designed products where real money changes hands and built mechanisms for trust between strangers
- Experience with international/localization challenges (we're in 190+ countries)
- Can articulate design decisions in terms of business impact, not just user experience
- Shows empathy for both sides of a marketplace in their design process
- Has worked closely with researchers and can speak to how insights shaped their design decisions

## Anti-Patterns

- Portfolio full of concept work or redesigns that never shipped
- Can't articulate the business rationale behind design decisions
- Views engineers as implementers rather than collaborators
- Designs that look good in Figma but ignore technical constraints or accessibility
- Treats localization as an afterthought rather than designing for global from the start

## Interview Focus

- Portfolio deep-dive: focus on process, constraints, tradeoffs, and how they measured success post-launch
- Two-sided thinking: how do their design decisions impact both hosts and guests
- System thinking: how they balance consistency with innovation when working within design systems
- Craft review with Brian: expect detailed feedback on visual hierarchy, information density, and interaction patterns
- Collaboration stories: how they work with PMs and engineers when there are competing priorities or technical constraints
