# What We're Looking For

We're not building another SaaS dashboard. Every line of code we ship touches real money — often life-changing amounts. A user's portfolio balance isn't just a number in a database; it's someone's retirement fund or their kid's college tuition. When our buy button breaks, people miss market opportunities. When our transaction history is wrong, it's not just confusing UX — it's a potential compliance violation.

I need someone who gets energized by that responsibility, not paralyzed by it. You should have opinions about crypto beyond "number go up." You don't need to be a DeFi yield farmer, but you should understand why decentralization matters and why we're building the infrastructure for an open financial system.

We move carefully but we still move. Our frontend needs to handle real-time price updates, complex transaction flows, and edge cases that traditional fintech never encounters. What happens when a transaction is pending on-chain for 2 hours? How do you show users their DeFi positions when the underlying protocol just upgraded? These aren't hypotheticals.

## Must-Haves

- Production React experience with financial or high-stakes applications (healthcare, payments, trading platforms)
- Deep understanding of async state management — you've built UIs that handle pending blockchain transactions, optimistic updates, and reconciliation
- Experience with real-time data (WebSockets, Server-Sent Events) — crypto prices and on-chain events don't wait
- Has written comprehensive frontend tests for money-touching features — unit, integration, and end-to-end
- Can articulate why you believe in cryptocurrency's potential beyond speculation

## Strong Signals

- Has built wallet integrations or worked with Web3 libraries (wagmi, ethers.js, web3.js)
- Experience with complex form validation for financial compliance (KYC, transaction limits, multi-step flows)
- Strong opinions about error handling and user communication during failures — "Something went wrong" isn't acceptable when someone's trying to buy Bitcoin
- Has worked in a regulated environment or understands the weight of financial compliance

## Anti-Patterns

- Crypto skeptic or thinks blockchain is just hype — you don't have to be a maximalist, but you need to believe in the mission
- Frontend experience only with CRUD apps or marketing sites — our state management challenges are unique
- Can't explain how they've handled edge cases with money or sensitive data
- Dismissive of testing culture or thinks end-to-end tests are too slow for their workflow
- Has never worked with real-time data or thinks "eventually consistent" is just a backend concern

## Interview Focus

- Crypto knowledge and mission alignment — not trivia, but genuine understanding of why this space matters
- How they've handled complex async flows, especially around money or high-stakes operations
- Testing philosophy for financial features — what they test, how they test, when they know they're done
- Real-time UX decisions — how do you show a user their transaction is pending when the blockchain is congested?
- Compliance mindset — have they built features where "move fast and break things" wasn't an option?
