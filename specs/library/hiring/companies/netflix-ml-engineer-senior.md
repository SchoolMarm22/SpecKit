# What We're Looking For

We're not looking for someone who can tune hyperparameters or run A/B tests. We need someone who can ship ML systems that serve 270M members across the globe, where a 0.1% improvement in engagement translates to millions in revenue. This isn't academic ML — it's ML at Netflix scale.

I want someone who's been through the full lifecycle: from messy data to production models to post-mortem analysis when the model started recommending weird stuff at 2am. You should have strong opinions about MLOps because you've been burned by bad deployment practices. You should understand that our recommendation engine isn't just an algorithm — it's a complex distributed system that needs to be reliable, interpretable, and constantly evolving.

Freedom and Responsibility means I'm not going to micromanage your feature engineering or model architecture choices. But it also means when you ship something that tanks user engagement, you own the fix. We hire senior people who can make good decisions with incomplete information.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped ML models to production that served millions of users daily
- Deep experience with at least one of: recommendation systems, ranking algorithms, or personalization at scale
- Built or significantly improved ML infrastructure (feature stores, model serving, experimentation platforms)
- Can code production-quality Python or Scala — not just notebook prototypes
- Has been on-call for ML systems and can tell war stories about model degradation

## Strong Signals

- Experience with large-scale distributed training (think billion-parameter models, not toy datasets)
- Built real-time ML systems with sub-100ms latency requirements
- Has strong opinions about feature engineering and can defend them
- Previous experience at a consumer internet company with personalization at scale
- Can articulate the business impact of their ML work in concrete metrics

## Anti-Patterns

- Academic ML background with no production experience (we're not a research lab)
- "Data scientist" experience that was mostly SQL and dashboards
- Can't explain why their model architecture choices were right for the business problem
- Treats ML as a black box — can't debug when models behave unexpectedly
- Has never had to explain to non-technical stakeholders why a model failed

## Interview Focus

- System design for ML at scale — how would you build Netflix recommendations from scratch?
- Real production ML war stories — what broke, how they debugged it, what they learned
- Business judgment — can they connect technical decisions to member satisfaction metrics?
- Coding interview focused on production ML code, not leetcode algorithms
- How they've navigated technical disagreements with other senior engineers
