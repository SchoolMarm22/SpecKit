# What We're Looking For

We're not building another generic project management tool. Linear exists because every other tool sacrifices speed for features, keyboard navigation for mouse-heavy workflows, and clean information architecture for enterprise checkbox features.

I need a designer who gets obsessed with the same things we do: 60fps animations that aren't gratuitous, keyboard shortcuts that feel like muscle memory, and visual hierarchy that works even when you're moving fast. If you've never written CSS or don't understand why bundle size affects design decisions, this isn't the role.

The best Linear features feel inevitable once you use them. That's not accident—it's the result of designers who think in systems, understand our users' actual workflows (not what they say they want), and can articulate why every pixel and interaction serves the product's core purpose.

## Must-Haves

- Designed and shipped B2B software that real developers use daily (not just pretty landing pages)
- Can implement your own designs in CSS/HTML to production quality—we pair with engineers, not hand off mockups
- Strong typography and information architecture skills proven in dense, data-heavy interfaces
- Track record of designing keyboard-first interactions and explaining why they matter
- Experience optimizing designs for performance constraints (understands how visual complexity affects render performance)

## Strong Signals

- Has strong opinions about developer tools UX backed by user research or personal experience
- Can critique Linear's current design decisions intelligently (what works, what doesn't, why)
- Prototypes interactions in code, not just Figma
- Has reduced cognitive load in complex workflows through design, not just made things "prettier"
- Experience with design systems that actually get adopted by engineering teams

## Anti-Patterns

- Portfolio heavy on consumer apps or marketing sites with little B2B product depth
- Can't explain design decisions beyond "it looks better" or "users prefer it"
- Treats accessibility as compliance rather than better design
- Expects engineers to figure out implementation details from static mockups
- Focuses on trends over user workflow optimization
- Has never worked closely with product engineering (always had a PM intermediary)

## Interview Focus

- Design critique of Linear itself—what would you change and why?
- Walk through a complex B2B workflow you've designed: how did you handle information density, progressive disclosure, keyboard navigation?
- Live design challenge: optimize an existing Linear flow for speed without losing functionality
- Technical collaboration: how do you work with engineers who care deeply about implementation details?
- Philosophy on animation and micro-interactions in productivity tools—when do they help vs. hinder?
