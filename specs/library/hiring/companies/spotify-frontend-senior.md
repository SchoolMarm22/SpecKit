# What We're Looking For

The Web Player squad owns one of the most critical touchpoints for Spotify users — the experience that plays music in browsers when the native apps aren't available. This isn't just another React app. We're dealing with Web Audio APIs, complex state synchronization across devices, offline playback, and the unique constraints of delivering audio in a browser environment.

Our squad operates with full autonomy — we own our roadmap, our tech choices, and our success metrics. You won't be implementing designs handed down from above. You'll be partnering with our designer and PM to figure out what to build, then building it. We ship to 400+ million users, and when our player breaks, people can't listen to music. The stakes are real.

## Must-Haves

- Deep experience with Web Audio API and media playback in browsers
- Built and owned a complex React application with real-time state management (Redux, Zustand, or similar)
- Experience with performance optimization for audio/media applications — can explain memory management, buffer strategies, and browser-specific quirks
- Has worked in an autonomous team environment where you influenced product decisions, not just implemented features
- Strong opinions about frontend architecture that come from experience, not blog posts

## Strong Signals

- Audio engineering background or deep interest in music technology
- Experience with cross-device synchronization or real-time applications
- Has built developer tooling or contributed to internal platforms (we love our Backstage contributors)
- Can talk intelligently about Web APIs beyond the standard DOM stuff — Service Workers, Web Workers, Streams API
- Previous experience at a consumer-facing product with millions of users

## Anti-Patterns

- Frontend engineers who avoid "the complex stuff" and stick to basic CRUD interfaces
- Can't articulate performance considerations beyond "make it fast"
- Expects product requirements to be fully specified before starting work
- Only comfortable working in homogeneous tech stacks (our backend is Java/Python, we interface with dozens of services)
- Treats accessibility as an afterthought (music should be for everyone)

## Interview Focus

- Technical deep-dive on media playback challenges — buffering strategies, format support, browser compatibility
- Autonomy and product sense — how they'd approach an ambiguous feature request
- Systems thinking — how they'd debug a cross-device sync issue
- Architecture decisions in their past work, especially around performance or real-time constraints
