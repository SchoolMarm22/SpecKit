# What We're Looking For

Search at Airbnb isn't just about finding a place to stay — it's about helping people discover experiences that create belonging. When someone searches for "Paris," they might be looking for a romantic getaway, a family adventure, or a solo journey of self-discovery. Our search has to understand intent, not just keywords.

You'll own the product vision for how 4 million hosts connect with travelers through our search and discovery experience. This means working closely with our design partners (design-driven culture is real here), understanding both sides of our marketplace, and obsessing over the craft details that make the difference between a good experience and a magical one. Brian cares deeply about these details, and so should you.

We rebuilt this team leaner after 2020. Every PM here owns more surface area and has more direct impact than they would at other companies. If you're looking for a role where you coordinate others' work, this isn't it. If you want to shape how millions of people discover their next adventure, let's talk.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped search, discovery, or marketplace products at consumer scale (10M+ users)
- Can articulate the difference between relevance, ranking, and personalization — and when each matters most
- Experience working with both design and ML/data science teams to balance user experience with algorithmic optimization
- Has made product decisions that improved marketplace liquidity or conversion for both sides
- Can talk through a complex product tradeoff using real data and user empathy

## Strong Signals

- Previous marketplace or two-sided platform experience (understands host/guest dynamics)
- Has shipped ML-powered product features end-to-end, not just "worked with ML teams"
- Strong design intuition — can articulate why a particular interaction pattern works or doesn't work
- Experience with international products (our search works differently in Tokyo vs. rural Montana)

## Anti-Patterns

- Treats search as just an engineering problem to be optimized
- Can't explain their product decisions in terms of real user value
- Has only worked on one side of a marketplace or never dealt with supply/demand imbalances
- Thinks A/B testing is sufficient for understanding user behavior (quantitative data without qualitative empathy)
- Views design as something that happens after the product is defined

## Interview Focus

- How they think about marketplace dynamics — what makes supply and demand healthy
- Real examples of balancing user experience against business metrics (not theoretical frameworks)
- Their process for working with designers — how they collaborate, not just coordinate
- Stories about shipping something that didn't work and how they diagnosed why (learning from failure)
- How they'd approach making search work for first-time vs. repeat users
