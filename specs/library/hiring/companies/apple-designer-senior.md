# What We're Looking For

We're not building for 10,000 users or even 100,000 users. We're building for hundreds of millions of people who will hold this in their hands every day. The difference between good and great at our scale is measured in muscle memory, in the split second of delight or frustration that defines someone's relationship with technology.

You'll be a DRI for experiences that span hardware and software in ways our competitors can't touch. The accelerometer data that tells us when to rotate the screen, the haptic engine that makes a button feel real, the way light hits the display — these aren't constraints, they're instruments. We need someone who thinks about design as a conversation between atoms and pixels.

You'll work in extraordinary secrecy on things that don't exist yet. You'll spend months perfecting an interaction that users experience for two seconds. You'll debate the philosophy of a corner radius with people who care as much as you do.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped consumer products that real people bought and used daily (not B2B tools, not internal dashboards)
- Prototyped with hardware constraints — weight, thermal, battery, physical buttons, sensors
- Experience designing across multiple input modalities (touch, voice, gesture, or hardware controls)
- Can articulate design decisions through the lens of human factors, not just aesthetic preference
- Portfolio shows evolution of ideas through iteration, not just final deliverables

## Strong Signals

- Has killed their own design work because it wasn't good enough, even when stakeholders were satisfied
- Thinks about accessibility as fundamental design quality, not compliance checklist
- Experience with manufacturing constraints or industrial design collaboration
- Can explain why they made specific decisions for left-handed users, or users with motor impairments, or users in direct sunlight

## Anti-Patterns

- Portfolio is all wireframes and flows with no evidence of pixel-perfect execution
- Describes user research as "validating designs" rather than discovering problems
- Can't explain the relationship between their design decisions and technical implementation
- Talks about "fast iteration" and "shipping fast" as primary values
- Experience is only in high-churn environments where design debt doesn't matter

## Interview Focus

- Hardware-software integration thinking: how does this design change if the device is in their pocket vs. their hand?
- Craft discussion: walk through their process for choosing specific type sizes, spacing, or interaction timing
- Constraint creativity: how do thermal limits or battery life influence interaction design decisions?
- Philosophy of simplicity: the difference between hiding complexity and solving for it
- Cross-functional collaboration: how they work with people they can't tell what they're building
