# What We're Looking For

We're not looking for someone who "facilitates alignment" or "drives cross-functional collaboration." That's table stakes. We need someone who can make hard product bets when the data is incomplete, because in Studio Technology, we're often building tools that don't exist anywhere else in the industry.

Our PM will own products that serve our content creators — directors, editors, VFX artists who are making shows watched by 260M+ members globally. These aren't internal tools someone begrudgingly uses. They're products that can make or break a $200M series. The stakes are real, the users are sophisticated, and "good enough" isn't good enough.

You'll work with engineers who've built systems at Netflix scale, but you'll also work with Academy Award winners who care more about milliseconds of playback latency than your OKRs. Both matter. If you can't translate between those worlds, you won't succeed here.

## Must-Haves

- Shipped B2B products where user adoption was genuinely optional and you had to earn it
- Made product decisions with incomplete data in a space where you couldn't just A/B test your way to the answer
- Worked directly with highly technical, opinionated users who could build their own solutions if yours wasn't good enough
- Can articulate specific technical tradeoffs in products you've shipped, not just business outcomes
- Experience with media/content workflows, real-time collaboration tools, or creative software

## Strong Signals

- Has killed a feature they personally championed because the data didn't support it
- Can explain why they chose specific technical approaches for product decisions (not just what the eng team told them)
- Experience with global products where latency, compliance, or scale constraints shaped core UX decisions
- Understands the difference between building for power users vs. trying to simplify everything

## Anti-Patterns

- Treats engineering as a "delivery team" rather than strategic partners
- Can't explain technical constraints that shaped their product decisions
- Product experience only with consumer apps or simple SaaS tools
- Uses "we'll test and iterate" as an answer to hard product questions
- Thinks good process can substitute for good judgment

## Interview Focus

- Specific product decisions they made that went against conventional wisdom and why
- How they've handled building products for users who are more expert than they are
- Technical depth: can they engage meaningfully when engineers push back on feasibility
- Real examples of when they've had to make bets with high uncertainty
- How they think about product quality vs. shipping velocity tradeoffs
